How to get to Milan

By plane, train or car — everything you need to plan your journey

Milan is one of the best-connected cities in Europe. It has three international airports, excellent high-speed rail links to the rest of Italy and Europe, and direct motorway connections from France, Switzerland and beyond. Here is everything you need to plan your journey.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Milan's three airports

Milan is served by three airports — each with different strengths and at different distances from the city centre. Choosing the right one can save you significant time and money.

By plane

From the United States

Delta Air Lines and United Airlines operate non-stop flights from New York JFK and Newark (EWR) to Milan Malpensa. From other US cities, connections are typically made through a European hub such as London Heathrow, Amsterdam Schiphol, Frankfurt or Paris CDG. All US long-haul flights arrive at Malpensa (MXP).

From the United Kingdom

Flights from the UK are frequent and often inexpensive. EasyJet and British Airways operate from London Heathrow and Gatwick to Malpensa and Linate. Ryanair operates from several UK regional airports to Bergamo Orio al Serio (BGY). The journey is typically 2h–2h20.

From Europe

Milan is well served by all major European carriers, as well as low-cost airlines including Ryanair, EasyJet, Vueling and Wizz Air. Malpensa receives most full-service carriers; Bergamo (BGY) is the primary low-cost hub. Linate handles many intra-European routes.

From Australia

No direct flights connect Australia with Milan. The most common routes involve a connection through the Middle East (Emirates, Etihad, Qatar Airways) or Southeast Asia (Singapore Airlines). Flights from Sydney, Melbourne or Perth typically take 22–28 hours including the layover.

By train from within Italy

Italy's high-speed rail network (Frecciarossa and Italo) connects Milan with the main Italian cities quickly and comfortably. Book in advance on Trenitalia or Italo for the best prices.

By train from Europe

Milan is well connected to European cities by international rail. TGV services operate from Paris Gare de Lyon to Milano Centrale in approximately 7 hours. EuroCity trains connect Milan with Zurich (3.5 hours), Geneva and other Swiss cities. Services also run from Barcelona via Genoa, and from Munich and Vienna via Verona.

By car

Several motorways converge on Milan from different directions. From the west (France/Switzerland): A8 and A26. From Switzerland (north): A9 via Como. From the east (Venice/Verona): A4. From the south (Bologna/Florence/Rome): A1. The ZTL (Limited Traffic Zone) restrictions in the city centre mean that hiring a car is most useful for exploring the surrounding region — not for accessing the centre itself. By bus

Long-distance bus companies including FlixBus and Eurolines connect Milan with major European and Italian cities. Buses are slower than trains but significantly cheaper, making them a good option for budget travellers. Most intercity buses arrive at Milano Lampugnano coach station, served by metro M1.

Frequently asked questions

Which airport should I use for Milan?+

Milan has three airports. Malpensa (MXP) is the main international airport, 40km from the centre — used by long-haul and most European carriers. Linate (LIN) is just 7km from the centre — most convenient if available. Bergamo Orio al Serio (BGY) is 47km from Milan and mainly served by Ryanair and other low-cost airlines.

How do I get from Malpensa Airport to Milan city centre?+

By train (Malpensa Express): to Milano Centrale (50 min, €13) or Milano Cadorna (30 min, €13). By bus (Terravision): to Centrale, 50 min, €10. By taxi: fixed price €100, ~50 min. By private transfer: booked in advance, door to hotel.

How do I get from Rome to Milan?+

High-speed trains (Frecciarossa, Italo) run between Roma Termini and Milano Centrale approximately every 30 minutes. Journey time is 2h55 to 3h30. Book in advance on trenitalia.com or italotreno.it for the best prices.

How do I get from Venice to Milan?+

High-speed trains run from Venezia Santa Lucia to Milano Centrale every 30 minutes. Journey time is approximately 2h15 to 2h30. Book on trenitalia.com or italotreno.it.

Are there direct flights from the US to Milan?+

Yes. Delta Air Lines operates non-stop from New York JFK, and United Airlines from Newark (EWR). From other US cities, connections go through a European hub. All US long-haul flights arrive at Malpensa (MXP).
