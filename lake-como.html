Lake Como

Bellagio, Varenna, Tremezzo — 50km from Milan, 1 hour by train

Lake Como (Lago di Como) is the most beautiful and celebrated of Italy's northern lakes — a dramatic Y-shaped expanse of deep blue water framed by the Alps and dotted with elegant villas, lush gardens and charming medieval villages. It is the most popular day trip from Milan for good reason.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026·⏱️ 4 min read

About Lake Como

Also known as Lake Lario (after its Latin name, Larius Lacus), Lake Como is the third largest lake in Italy and the deepest, with a maximum depth of 410 metres. Its distinctive shape — an inverted Y formed by three long arms — has the town of Como to the west, Lecco to the east and Colico to the north. The central junction, where all three arms meet, is occupied by Bellagio.

The lake has been a retreat for the Roman aristocracy, Renaissance princes and modern celebrities alike. The villas and gardens that line its shores are among the most spectacular in Italy — many built during the 18th and 19th centuries by the Lombard nobility who came here to escape the summer heat of Milan.

The villages

Each of Lake Como's villages has its own character and charm:

⭐ Bellagio
Lake Como's most celebrated village, at the junction of the three arms. Steep, narrow streets, spectacular lake views in every direction, and some of the finest waterfront terraces on the lake. The unmissable stop.
🌊 Varenna
A quieter, more authentic alternative to Bellagio — a small medieval hamlet with scenic walkways built above the water and a labyrinth of steep stairs climbing the hillside. Especially beautiful in the evening.
🌸 Tremezzo
Home to Villa Carlotta — a remarkable neoclassical residence given as a wedding gift to Princess Charlotte of Prussia in 1843, with some of the most spectacular gardens on the lake.
🏰 Menaggio
A beautifully preserved medieval town with an old castle and grand lakeside villas. Excellent base for exploring the western shore and crossing to Bellagio by ferry.
🏙️ Como
The main city at the southern tip of the western arm. Founded by the Romans in 196 BC. Take the funicular to Brunate for breathtaking views over the lake and city.
⛵ Getting around
The ferry (traghetto) is the best and most enjoyable way to move between villages. Car ferries also run for those who want to take their vehicle across the lake.

Visiting tips

  • Best time to visit: May–June and September–October offer the best weather with fewer crowds. July and August are beautiful but very busy.
  • From Como, take the funicular to Brunate (about 7 minutes) for the best panoramic views of the lake and the Alps.
  • If visiting Bellagio and Varenna, travel between them by ferry — it's the most scenic and practical option.
  • Book Villa Carlotta in Tremezzo in advance if visiting in spring, when the gardens are at their best.
  • Ferry timetables are available at any landing stage and can be checked at navigazionelaghi.it.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Milan to Lake Como?+

By train: Cadorna → Como Nord Lago, ~1 hour, €5.20 one way, every 30 minutes from 7:13am. By car: A9 motorway north, ~50 km, 45 minutes. By guided tour: several day trips from Milan include Bellagio, Varenna and more.

What is the best village to visit on Lake Como?+

Bellagio is the most famous and most beautiful — at the junction of the lake's three arms, with spectacular views in every direction. Varenna is a quieter, more authentic alternative. Tremezzo is famous for Villa Carlotta and its gardens.

How far is Lake Como from Milan?+

Lake Como is approximately 50 km north of Milan — about 45 minutes by car on the A9 motorway, or 1 hour by train from Cadorna station.

What is the best time to visit Lake Como?+

May–June and September–October offer the best combination of good weather, manageable crowds and scenic beauty. July and August are the busiest months but also the warmest for swimming.
