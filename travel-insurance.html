Travel Insurance for Milan

What cover you need for visiting Italy — EHIC/GHIC, recommended policies and emergency contacts

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Do I need travel insurance for Milan?

Travel insurance is not legally required to enter Italy, but it is strongly recommended. Medical treatment in Italy can be expensive for non-EU visitors, and even for EU/UK citizens, the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC/GHIC) only covers emergency state healthcare — not repatriation, trip cancellation, lost luggage or other common travel issues.

European Health Insurance Card (EHIC/GHIC)

EU citizens should carry their EHIC (European Health Insurance Card), which entitles you to state healthcare in Italy at the same rate as Italian residents. This is free to obtain from your national health authority.

UK citizens should carry the GHIC (Global Health Insurance Card) — the post-Brexit equivalent of the EHIC, which provides equivalent cover in Italy. Apply for free via the NHS website. Note: the GHIC is not a substitute for comprehensive travel insurance.

What travel insurance should cover

  • Medical expenses and hospitalisation — the most important element for non-EU visitors
  • Emergency medical repatriation — costly without insurance
  • Trip cancellation and curtailment
  • Lost, stolen or damaged luggage
  • Travel delays
  • Personal liability

Emergency contacts in Milan

  • 112 — Universal EU emergency number (police, ambulance, fire)
  • 118 — Medical emergency / ambulance
  • Policlinico di Milano — Major public hospital: Via Francesco Sforza, 35
  • Ospedale Niguarda — Major public hospital: Piazza dell'Ospedale Maggiore, 3

In a medical emergency, call 118 for an ambulance or go directly to the Pronto Soccorso (A&E) of the nearest hospital.

What to do in a medical emergency in Milan

In a medical emergency, call 118 for an ambulance. Ambulances in Italy are free at the point of use for emergency calls. For non-emergency medical advice, pharmacies (identifiable by a green cross) can be consulted without an appointment — pharmacists are qualified to advise on minor illnesses and can dispense many medications without a prescription.

If you need to visit an emergency department (Pronto Soccorso), the two main public hospitals are Policlinico di Milano (Via Francesco Sforza, 35, M3 Crocetta) and Ospedale Niguarda (Piazza Ospedale Maggiore, 3, north of the city centre). EU citizens will be treated at the same cost as Italian nationals upon presentation of their EHIC card. Non-EU visitors without insurance will be charged at private rates.

EHIC and GHIC in detail

The European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) entitles EU citizens to access Italian state healthcare on the same basis as Italian nationals — this means emergency treatment is either free or at the same reduced cost that Italians pay. Apply for free from your national health authority before travelling. The card covers emergency treatment, essential care during pregnancy, and treatment for chronic conditions — but not planned treatment, private healthcare or medical repatriation.

UK citizens should apply for the Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC), which replaced the EHIC after Brexit and provides equivalent cover in Italy and other EU countries. Apply for free via the NHS website. Important: the GHIC does not cover medical repatriation to the UK, which can cost tens of thousands of pounds — this must be covered by a separate travel insurance policy.

Travel insurance checklist for Italy

A comprehensive travel insurance policy for visiting Italy should include: emergency medical treatment (at least €500,000 cover), emergency repatriation, trip cancellation and curtailment (including cancellation due to illness), lost, stolen or damaged luggage and personal items, travel delays, personal liability (in case you accidentally injure someone or damage property) and — if relevant — sports and activities cover for skiing, cycling or other activities in the surrounding mountains.

Check whether your policy covers pre-existing medical conditions — many standard policies exclude these unless declared and an additional premium paid. If you are taking day trips to Switzerland (Lake Maggiore extends into Switzerland; St Moritz trips are popular from Milan), ensure your policy covers non-EU destinations.

Stolen documents and the consulate

If your passport is stolen, report the theft immediately to the nearest police station (Questura) — you need a police report (denuncia di furto) for both insurance purposes and to obtain emergency travel documents. Your nearest consulate or embassy can issue an Emergency Travel Document (ETD) to allow you to return home. The British Consulate in Milan is at Via San Paolo, 7. The US Consulate General is at Via Principe Amedeo, 2/10.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need travel insurance to visit Italy?+

Travel insurance is not legally required to enter Italy, but it is strongly recommended — especially for non-EU visitors. Medical treatment without insurance can be expensive. Even EU/UK citizens with EHIC/GHIC cards should have separate travel insurance covering repatriation, cancellation and luggage, as EHIC/GHIC does not cover these.

Does my EHIC card cover me in Italy?+

Yes — an EHIC (EU) or GHIC (UK) card entitles you to state healthcare in Italy on the same basis as Italian nationals. This covers emergency treatment and essential care. It does not cover private healthcare, medical repatriation, or non-emergency treatment. Always carry the physical card, not just a digital copy.

What number do I call for an ambulance in Milan?+

Call 118 for an ambulance in Milan. This is the Italian medical emergency number, free to call. For all emergencies (police, fire, ambulance) you can also call the EU universal emergency number 112.

Does travel insurance cover trip cancellation for any reason?+

Standard travel insurance covers cancellation due to illness (yours or a close relative's), death, redundancy and a defined list of events. 'Cancel for any reason' (CFAR) is a separate, more expensive policy add-on. Check the specific exclusions in your policy before purchasing — pandemic-related cancellations, for example, may require specific cover.

What hospitals are near the centre of Milan?+

The two main public hospitals with 24-hour emergency departments near the centre are Policlinico di Milano (Via Francesco Sforza, 35 — M3 Crocetta) and Ospedale Fatebenefratelli (Corso di Porta Nuova, 23). Ospedale Niguarda is the largest hospital in Milan and is located north of the centre.
