Bergamo Orio al Serio Airport (BGY)

Main low-cost hub for Milan — Ryanair & others · 47km from Milan · 4km from Bergamo

Bergamo Orio al Serio Airport (BGY), also known as Il Caravaggio International Airport, is the main low-cost airport for the Milan area and one of the busiest airports in Italy, handling over 13 million passengers per year. It is the primary Italian base for Ryanair and several other low-cost carriers.

How to get from Bergamo Airport to Milan

🚌
Coach to Milano Centrale Best value
~50 min · €10 one way · €18 return
Terravision and Autostradale coaches run between Bergamo Airport and Milano Centrale (Piazza Luigi da Savoia) approximately every 30 minutes, around the clock. Journey time: approximately 50 minutes. Price: €10 one way, €18 return (children €5). Book in advance on terravision.eu for the best price. The Orio Shuttle also runs every 30 minutes (3:50am–1:30am) at a similar price. This is by far the most popular and practical way to get to Milan from BGY.
🚂
Bus to Bergamo station → train to Milan
~1h15 total · ~€9 combined · see Bergamo en route
There is no railway station at Bergamo Airport. However, you can take a local bus to Bergamo city train station (about 15 minutes) and then a direct train to Milano Centrale (less than 1 hour, ~€6). The total journey is longer than the direct coach, but it lets you stop in Bergamo's beautiful Città Alta before continuing to Milan — an excellent option if you arrive early and want to explore.
🚕
Taxi to Milan
€100+ · ~50 min · not recommended
A taxi from Bergamo Airport to Milan city centre costs over €100 and takes approximately 50 minutes. Given the excellent coach service at €10, a taxi is rarely worth the cost unless you have a very specific requirement — large group, late-night arrival with heavy luggage, or a destination outside central Milan.

Visiting Bergamo from the airport

One of the hidden advantages of landing at BGY is that Bergamo city centre (Città Alta) is just 4km away. If you have time before your flight or arrive ahead of schedule, the medieval upper city is one of the finest day trips from Milan — and from this airport, it's effectively on your doorstep.

💡 Travel tip

If you fly into Bergamo Airport and your hotel is in Milan, consider spending a few hours in Bergamo's Città Alta before taking the coach to Milan. Leave your luggage in storage at the airport or at Bergamo train station, explore the walled upper city, then take the coach to Milan in the evening. It costs nothing extra and Bergamo is genuinely exceptional. Bergamo day trip guide →

About Bergamo Airport

Bergamo Airport is officially named Il Caravaggio International Airport, after the painter Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio, who was born near Bergamo in 1571. Despite its name and its location in Bergamo province, the airport is commercially marketed as a Milan airport — it is used primarily by travellers going to or from Milan, for whom the 47km distance is acceptable given the much lower fares that low-cost carriers offer from BGY compared to Malpensa.

The airport has a single terminal. Facilities are functional rather than luxurious, with the usual range of food outlets, shops and car rental desks. A direct connection to Bergamo city centre has improved significantly in recent years with dedicated bus links.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Bergamo Airport to Milan?+

The best option is the Terravision or Autostradale coach to Milano Centrale — approximately 50 minutes, €10 one way, every 30 minutes 24 hours a day. A taxi costs over €100. Private transfers available at a fixed price.

How far is Bergamo Airport from Milan?+

Bergamo Airport is approximately 47km (29 miles) east of Milan city centre. The journey by coach takes about 50 minutes; by car approximately 50 minutes on the A4 motorway.

Which airlines fly to Bergamo Airport?+

Bergamo Airport is the primary Italian base for Ryanair, plus other low-cost and charter airlines. Full-service carriers (British Airways, Lufthansa, Air France) fly to Malpensa or Linate instead.

Can I visit Bergamo from the airport?+

Yes — Bergamo city centre (Città Alta) is just 4km from the airport, easily reached by local bus. The medieval upper city is outstanding and well worth the detour, especially if you have time before your flight or on arrival.

Is there a train from Bergamo Airport to Milan?+

There is no railway station at the airport. You can take a bus to Bergamo train station (15 min) then a train to Milano Centrale (~1h, €6) — slower than the direct coach but it lets you visit Bergamo en route.
