General Information about Milan

Essential facts, currency, language, emergency numbers and practical tips

Milan (Milano in Italian) is Italy's second most populous city and one of the world's great centres of fashion, design and finance. Here is everything you need to know before your visit.

Currency & payments

Italy uses the euro (€). ATMs are widely available throughout Milan — the most cost-effective way to get cash is usually to withdraw from a bank ATM using your debit card. Credit and debit cards are accepted in most restaurants, hotels and shops, though some smaller trattorias and markets still prefer cash. Contactless payments are widely accepted.

Language

The official language is Italian. In tourist areas, hotels and larger restaurants, English is spoken to a reasonable standard. Outside the centre, English is less common — knowing a few basic Italian phrases is appreciated. Language guide for Milan →

Tipping

Tipping is not obligatory in Italy. In restaurants, a coperto (cover charge, €1.50–€3 per person) is standard and is already included in the bill. If service has been exceptional, leaving a small tip (€2–5) is appreciated but not expected. In taxis, rounding up the fare is common.

Safety

Milan is a safe city for tourists. The main concerns are pickpocketing in busy tourist areas (around the Duomo, on the metro) and unlicensed taxi drivers at airports. Keep bags close, be aware in crowded spaces, and only use official white taxis. See our travel advice page for more detail.

Emergency numbers

  • 112 — EU universal emergency number (police, ambulance, fire)
  • 113 — Italian police (Polizia di Stato)
  • 115 — Fire brigade
  • 118 — Medical emergency / ambulance
  • 116 — ACI roadside assistance

About Milan

Milan (Milano in Italian) is the capital of Lombardy and Italy's second most populous city, with approximately 1.4 million inhabitants in the city proper and 3.2 million in the wider metropolitan area. It is Italy's undisputed economic capital — home to the Milan Stock Exchange (Borsa Italiana), the headquarters of most major Italian corporations, two of Italy's largest banks, and a global centre for fashion, design and publishing.

Geographically, Milan sits in the Po Valley — the flat agricultural plain of northern Italy — at an altitude of 120 metres above sea level. The Alps are visible on clear days to the north and northwest. The city is roughly equidistant from the major northern Italian lakes: Lake Como (50km), Lake Maggiore (80km) and Lake Garda (140km).

Useful apps for visiting Milan

The ATM Milano app allows you to buy and validate public transport tickets on your phone — no need to carry paper tickets. Google Maps works well for navigation and includes real-time metro and tram timetables. Trenitalia and Italo apps are essential for booking high-speed train tickets to other Italian cities. TheFork (ElTenedor) is useful for restaurant reservations. The Civitatis app allows you to book tours and activities.

Wi-Fi and connectivity

Free Wi-Fi is available in most hotels, and increasingly in cafés and restaurants. The city of Milan provides free public Wi-Fi in some public areas. Mobile data roaming within the EU is included in most European phone plans at no extra charge. US and other non-EU visitors should check their carrier's roaming rates or consider purchasing a local SIM card on arrival — available from phone shops and some supermarkets.

Accessibility

Milan has made significant progress in accessibility but remains imperfect. The metro system has lifts at many but not all stations — check the ATM website for the current status. The historic centre's uneven cobblestone streets and the lack of dropped kerbs in some areas can be challenging for wheelchair users. Many museums and monuments have wheelchair access, though some historic buildings have limitations. The accessible tourism portal turismomilano.it has detailed information.

Frequently asked questions

What is Milan famous for?+

Milan is famous for fashion (Gucci, Prada, Versace, Armani all have their headquarters or flagship stores here), the Salone del Mobile design fair (the world's most important design event), the Gothic Cathedral (Duomo), Leonardo da Vinci's Last Supper, and as Italy's financial and business capital. It also has one of the world's great opera houses, Teatro alla Scala.

What currency is used in Milan?+

Italy uses the euro (€). ATMs (Bancomat) are widely available throughout Milan. Cards are accepted in most establishments, but carrying some cash for small purchases, markets and some smaller restaurants is advisable.

What time zone is Milan in?+

Milan is in the Central European Time zone: UTC+1 in winter (CET) and UTC+2 in summer (CEST, from late March to late October). It is 1 hour ahead of the UK in winter and the same time as the UK in summer, and 6 hours ahead of New York in winter.

What is the population of Milan?+

Milan has approximately 1.4 million inhabitants in the city proper, making it Italy's second largest city after Rome. The wider metropolitan area has approximately 3.2 million people, and the broader Lombardy region — of which Milan is the capital — has over 10 million.

What plug type is used in Italy?+

Italy uses Type C (two round pins) and Type L (three round pins in a line) plugs. The voltage is 230V at 50Hz. UK visitors need a plug adapter; US and Canadian visitors need both an adapter and a voltage converter for devices that do not support 220-240V.
