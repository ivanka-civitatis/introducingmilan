Pinacoteca Ambrosiana

The Pinacoteca Ambrosiana is one of the finest art museums in Milan — and one of the least crowded. Founded in 1618 by Cardinal Federico Borromeo, it houses masterpieces by Leonardo da Vinci, Caravaggio, Botticelli and Titian, and is home to the largest collection of Leonardo's Codex Atlanticus in the world.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo · Updated June 2026 · ⏱️ 4 min read

About the museum

Cardinal Federico Borromeo founded the Pinacoteca Ambrosiana in 1618 with a clear and remarkable purpose: to inspire and support future generations of artists by providing them with access to great works of art. To this end he also founded the Biblioteca Ambrosiana and the Accademia del Disegno — a drawing academy — where young Counter-Reformation artists could study.

The gallery is housed in a remarkable palazzo built in 1928, attached to the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, just steps from Piazza Mercanti and the Duomo. Despite its extraordinary collection, it remains significantly less visited than the Pinacoteca di Brera — which means you can often enjoy the masterpieces in near-solitude.

The four masterpieces

The gallery contains an exceptional collection of over 400 works spanning the 13th to the 20th centuries. The four most celebrated are:

Sandro Botticelli
Madonna del Padiglione
Madonna and Child with Three Angels — a jewel-like tondo painted around 1493, one of Botticelli's most tender compositions.
Caravaggio
Basket of Fruit (Canestra di frutta)
Painted around 1599, this is considered the first pure still life in Western art — a revolutionary work by the young Caravaggio.
Leonardo da Vinci
Portrait of a Musician
The only known portrait by Leonardo of a male subject, and the only Leonardo in Milan outside of the Last Supper.
Titian (Tiziano)
Adoration of the Magi
A rich, monumental altarpiece from the 1560s — one of Titian's most ambitious late compositions.

The Biblioteca Ambrosiana and the Codex Atlanticus

The Biblioteca Ambrosiana, founded in 1609, was the second public library inaugurated in Europe. Today it holds over 750,000 books and 35,000 manuscripts — an extraordinary collection spanning centuries of European thought.

Its most prized possession is the Codex Atlanticus — over 1,000 pages of drawings and writings by Leonardo da Vinci, covering an astonishing range of subjects: machines, weapons, musical instruments, mathematics, botany, optics and anatomy. It is the largest collection of Leonardo's writings in the world, and seeing it in person is one of the most moving experiences any museum in Milan can offer.

📚 The Codex Atlanticus

The Codex Atlanticus takes its name from its enormous format — the pages are so large they were compared to atlases. It was compiled by the sculptor Pompeo Leoni in the late 16th century, who bound together over 1,000 of Leonardo's loose drawings and notes. A small section is also displayed in the Bramante Sacristy at Santa Maria delle Grazie, next to the Last Supper.

Visiting tips

  • The museum is closed on Wednesdays — plan your visit for any other day of the week.
  • Allow at least 2 hours for the gallery alone, and an additional hour if you want to explore the Biblioteca Ambrosiana.
  • The Portrait of a Musician by Leonardo is the only Leonardo portrait of a male subject in existence — don't miss it.
  • The Caravaggio Basket of Fruit is considered the first true still life in Western art history — context that makes it far more powerful to see in person.
  • Combine with nearby Piazza Mercanti (182 metres) and the Duomo (383 metres) for a full morning in the historic centre.

Frequently asked questions

What are the opening hours of the Pinacoteca Ambrosiana?+

Open Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday and Sunday from 10am to 6pm. Closed on Wednesdays.

How much does it cost to visit the Pinacoteca Ambrosiana?+

Gallery ticket: Adults €17, over 65s €13, students and 15–18 year-olds €10, children 6–14 €5, under 5s free. Combined gallery + crypt: Adults €20, concessions €15.

What is the Codex Atlanticus?+

The Codex Atlanticus is the largest collection of drawings and writings by Leonardo da Vinci in existence — over 1,000 pages covering machines, weapons, musical instruments, mathematics, botany and more. The original is housed in the Biblioteca Ambrosiana at the Pinacoteca.

What masterpieces can you see at the Pinacoteca Ambrosiana?+

The four most celebrated works are: Madonna del Padiglione (Botticelli), Basket of Fruit (Caravaggio — the first true still life in Western art), Portrait of a Musician (Leonardo da Vinci — the only Leonardo portrait of a male subject), and Adoration of the Magi (Titian).

How do I get to the Pinacoteca Ambrosiana?+

By metro: Duomo (lines M1 and M3) or Cordusio (line M1). By tram: lines 2, 3, 12, 14 and 16. The gallery is at Piazza Pio XI, 2, a 5-minute walk from the Duomo.
