Day Trips from Milan

The Italian Lakes, medieval cities and Alpine scenery — all within 1.5 hours of the city

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

One of Milan's greatest advantages for travellers is its position at the heart of northern Italy. Within 90 minutes you can be on the shore of one of Europe's most beautiful lakes, in a UNESCO-listed medieval city, or boarding a mountain train bound for Switzerland. Milan is an exceptional base for day trips.

The Italian Lakes

The three great northern Italian lakes — Como, Maggiore and Garda — are all reachable from Milan in under 90 minutes. Each has a distinct character: Como is the most dramatic and celebrated; Maggiore is the most romantic, with its Baroque island gardens; Garda is the most lively, with beaches and theme parks. A fourth option, Lake Iseo, is smaller, quieter and largely undiscovered by international tourists.

Historic cities

Northern Italy's historic cities are among the most rewarding destinations in Europe, and most are within easy reach of Milan. Bergamo's Città Alta is one of the finest medieval city centres in Italy; Verona (reachable from Lake Garda) has the Roman Arena and Juliet's balcony; Mantua (Mantova) is a jewel of the Renaissance largely undiscovered by British and American tourists.

Getting there

The lakes are best reached by car for flexibility, but excellent public transport options exist for the most popular destinations. Lake Como is served directly by train from Cadorna (1 hour, €5.20); Bergamo from Centrale or Garibaldi (under 1 hour, €6). For Lake Maggiore, the train from Cadorna reaches Laveno in 1.5 hours. Guided day trips handle everything — transport, ferry tickets and guide — for those who prefer not to navigate independently.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best day trip from Milan?+

Lake Como is the most celebrated and most scenic — Bellagio is particularly beautiful. For a different experience, Bergamo's medieval Città Alta is one of the finest in northern Italy and reachable in 45 minutes by train. Lake Maggiore (Isola Bella) is excellent if you are interested in Baroque gardens.

Can you do Lake Como as a day trip from Milan?+

Yes — Lake Como is only 50km from Milan and reachable in 1 hour by train from Cadorna station (€5.20 one way). A day trip allows time for Bellagio, a ferry ride and lunch. May, June, September and October are the best months to visit.

How do I get from Milan to the lakes?+

Lake Como: train from Cadorna (1h, €5.20). Lake Maggiore: train from Cadorna to Laveno (1.5h, €8) or car on the A8 (1h). Lake Garda: car on the motorway direction Verona (1.5h) or train to Desenzano (50min–1.5h, €11–31). Lake Iseo: car (~1h). See individual lake guides for details.
