How far in advance should I book?

The Last Supper should be booked as soon as you know your travel dates — it regularly sells out 2–3 months in advance. A reasonable rule: book the Last Supper the day you book your flights. The Duomo rooftop can be booked a few days ahead; the Pinacoteca di Brera and most other museums do not require advance booking except on free Sunday days. If visiting during Fashion Week (February or September) or the Salone del Mobile (April), book accommodation 3–6 months in advance — prices triple and availability disappears.

How many days do you need in Milan?

1 day: Duomo and rooftop, Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, La Scala, lunch in Brera, Pinacoteca di Brera in the afternoon, aperitivo in Navigli. You will miss a great deal but will see the essentials.

2 days: Add the Last Supper, Castello Sforzesco, Piazza Mercanti, and one of the free museums (GAM or Cimitero Monumentale). This covers the main highlights well. See our complete 2-day itinerary.

3 days: Adds a day trip (Lake Como is the natural choice) and time to explore a neighbourhood properly — Isola, Porta Venezia or the Navigli area at leisure.

4+ days: You can cover every major sight, take two day trips, visit smaller museums and genuinely get under the skin of the city.

Milan as a base for northern Italy

Milan's central position in northern Italy makes it an exceptional base. Within 90 minutes you can reach Lake Como, Lake Maggiore, Lake Garda, Bergamo, Brescia, the Valtellina ski resorts, and — with a high-speed train — Verona, Venice (2h15), Florence (1h45) and Bologna (1h). Many visitors use Milan as their starting point for a broader northern Italian itinerary, spending 2–3 days in the city before continuing east or south by train.

Frequently asked questions

How many days do I need in Milan?+

Two days covers the main highlights well: the Duomo, Last Supper, Castello Sforzesco, Pinacoteca di Brera, and an evening in the Navigli. Three days adds a day trip to Lake Como. Four days allows a more relaxed pace with time for neighbourhood exploration and secondary museums.

When is the best time to book the Last Supper?+

Book as early as possible — ideally on the same day you book your flights. The Cenacolo Vinciano regularly sells out 2–3 months in advance, especially in spring and summer. If standard tickets are sold out, guided tours that include entry are often available when direct tickets are not.

Is Milan a good base for visiting other Italian cities?+

Yes — Milan's high-speed rail connections make it an excellent base. Venice is 2h15 by train, Florence 1h45, Bologna 1h, Rome under 3h. For northern Italy, Lake Como is 1h by train, Bergamo 45 minutes, Verona 1h15. Many visitors combine Milan with 1–2 other cities on the same trip.

What should I prioritise if I only have one day in Milan?+

With one day: start early at the Duomo (interior free, rooftop booked in advance), walk through the Galleria to Piazza della Scala, have lunch in Brera, visit the Pinacoteca di Brera in the afternoon (2–3 hours), and end with aperitivo in the Navigli. If you have the Last Supper pre-booked, replace the afternoon with a visit to Castello Sforzesco and the Last Supper.
