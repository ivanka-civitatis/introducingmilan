Milan Sightseeing Bus

Hop-on hop-off · Duomo, Castello, Navigli & more · Audio guide in 8 languages

Milan's City Sightseeing hop-on hop-off bus offers a practical way to see the city's main attractions aboard an open-top double-decker, with a pre-recorded audio guide available in up to 8 languages. Useful for first-time visitors who want a quick overview before exploring on foot.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Main stops

⛪ Milan Cathedral (Duomo)
The starting point for most routes — the Duomo and surrounding piazza are the natural heart of any sightseeing tour of Milan.
🏰 Castello Sforzesco
The 15th-century Sforza castle and the entrance to Parco Sempione — a 10-minute walk from the Duomo but a natural hop-off point.
🎨 Santa Maria delle Grazie
Home of Leonardo da Vinci's Last Supper — a key stop for visitors who want to see the church exterior (tickets for the Last Supper are separate and must be pre-booked).
🚢 Navigli
Milan's canal district — the most atmospheric neighbourhood in the city for an evening aperitivo or dinner, and usually covered on the main sightseeing route.
🚂 Milano Centrale
The main railway station — a stop useful if you are heading elsewhere after the tour.
📍 Piazza della Repubblica
The elegant square between Centrale and the historic centre — useful for hotels in the Porta Venezia area.

Tickets and practical information

Adult tickets start from approximately €29 for a 24-hour pass. Children's tickets are available at reduced prices. Buses run daily from approximately 9:30am, with the last departure in the early evening. Frequency is typically every 20–30 minutes at peak times.

Tickets can be bought on board, online in advance (recommended in high season) or at authorised points of sale near the Duomo. Check the City Sightseeing Milan website for current prices, timetables and to book online.

💡 Is it worth it?

The sightseeing bus is genuinely useful if you have only a few hours in Milan and want a quick overview of the main sights before choosing where to explore further. For visitors staying two or more days, the metro and walking are more flexible and usually more enjoyable. The bus is also a good option if you are travelling with elderly relatives or anyone who struggles with a lot of walking.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Milan sightseeing bus worth it?+

Useful for first-time visitors with limited time who want a quick overview. For longer stays, the metro and walking give more flexibility.

How much does the Milan sightseeing bus cost?+

Adult tickets from approximately €29 for a 24-hour pass. Check City Sightseeing Milan for current prices and to book in advance.

What are the main stops on the Milan sightseeing bus?+

Duomo, Castello Sforzesco, Santa Maria delle Grazie (Last Supper), Navigli, Milano Centrale and Piazza della Repubblica. Check the current route map as lines may vary by season.
