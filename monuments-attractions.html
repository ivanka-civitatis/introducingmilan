Monuments & Tourist Attractions in Milan

Churches, palaces, piazzas, parks and historic sites — the complete guide

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Milan's monuments span 2,600 years of history — from the visible remains of Roman walls and a 4th-century basilica to a 15th-century fortress, a 19th-century arcade and a 21st-century district of futurist towers. Whether you are interested in medieval piazzas, Gothic cathedrals or contemporary architecture, the city rewards exploration.

Major monuments

Milan's most celebrated monuments are concentrated in a compact historic centre walkable in its entirety. The Duomo anchors the east; Castello Sforzesco and the Last Supper anchor the west. In between lies everything else.

Historic churches

Milan's churches are some of the most rewarding and least-visited sights in the city. Most are free to enter. The combination of San Maurizio al Monastero Maggiore (extraordinary frescoes) and the adjacent Milan Archaeological Museum (Roman walls) makes for one of the finest free mornings in the city.

Basilica · Free
Basilica di Sant'Ambrogio
Milan's patron saint · built 387 AD · crypt with 3 saints · Romanesque masterpiece
Church · Free

Parks & outdoor spaces

Milan is greener than many visitors expect. Parco Sempione (47 hectares behind the castle) is the obvious choice for a break from sightseeing; the Giardini Pubblici near Porta Venezia is more intimate and excellent for families.

Park · Free
Parco Sempione
Milan's largest central park · Arco della Pace · Triennale di Milano · free
Park · Free
Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli
Porta Venezia · natural history museum · playground · adjacent to the GAM

Modern Milan

The Porta Nuova development around Piazza Gae Aulenti has transformed a former railway yard into Milan's most striking modern district — a forest of glass towers that represents a completely different side of the city from its historic centre. Worth a visit, especially at night when the illuminated towers are reflected in the fountains.

Frequently asked questions

What are the main monuments in Milan?+

Milan's main monuments are the Gothic Cathedral (Duomo), the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, Castello Sforzesco, the Last Supper (book in advance), Teatro alla Scala, Cimitero Monumentale and the medieval Piazza Mercanti. For churches: Sant'Ambrogio, San Maurizio al Monastero Maggiore and the Basilica of San Lorenzo.

Which Milan monuments are free to visit?+

The Duomo interior, Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, Piazza Mercanti, Cimitero Monumentale, Parco Sempione, Piazza Gae Aulenti, Sant'Ambrogio, San Maurizio al Monastero Maggiore, San Bernardino alle Ossa and the Castello Sforzesco courtyard are all free.
