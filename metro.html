Milan Metro

5 lines · 113 stations · 94.5km · Italy's longest network · €2.20 per journey

The Milan Metro (Metropolitana di Milano) is Italy's longest metro network — 94.5km of track, 5 colour-coded lines and 113 stations across the city and into the metropolitan area. Operated by ATM, it runs from 6am to 12:30am daily and a single journey costs €2.20.

The five metro lines

M1
Red Line — Sesto FS / Rho Fieramilano ↔ Bisceglie / Assago
The most useful line for tourists and business travellers alike. Key stops: Duomo, San Babila (change for M4 to Linate), Cadorna (Malpensa Express train), Loreto (change for M2), Fiera Milano. Also serves Monza via suburban rail integration.
M2
Green Line — Cologno Nord / Gessate ↔ Abbiategrasso / Assago
Key stops: Milano Centrale, Garibaldi (Gae Aulenti area), Loreto (change for M1), Cadorna, Porta Genova (Navigli district), Sant'Ambrogio. Useful for Bergamo and Linate rail connections at Lambrate.
M3
Yellow Line — Comasina ↔ San Donato
Key stops: Duomo, Montenapoleone (fashion district), Milano Centrale, Repubblica, Lanza (Brera district). Convenient for visitors staying between Centrale and the Duomo — these two stops are just 3 stations apart on M3.
M4
Blue Line — Linate Airport ↔ San Cristoforo · NEW 2023
Opened in 2023 — the newest addition to the network. Key stops: Linate Airport, Repaci, Forlanini FS, Dateo, San Babila (change for M1), Sforza Policlinico, Sant'Ambrogio, San Cristoforo. Travel time from Linate to San Babila: ~15 minutes. Ticket: €2.20 (standard ATM ticket).
M5
Purple Line — Bignami ↔ San Siro Stadio
Key stops: Garibaldi (change for M2), Monumentale, Zara, San Siro Stadio (for football and concerts). Integrates with suburban rail at Garibaldi and Zara. Essential for anyone attending events at the San Siro stadium.

Tickets and prices

All ATM tickets are integrated — a single ticket is valid for 90 minutes on metro, tram and bus, allowing one metro journey plus unlimited transfers on surface transport. Validate your ticket before boarding — inspectors operate on all lines and fines are substantial.

Tickets can be bought at metro station vending machines, newsagents and tobacconists (tabaccherie) displaying the ATM logo, and via the ATM Milano app on your phone. Note: buying a ticket on board a tram or bus incurs a supplement — always buy in advance.

⚠️ Validate before boarding

Always validate your ticket at the yellow machines at the metro gate or at the validator on trams and buses before boarding. Travelling without a validated ticket is subject to a fine of €60–€100, regardless of whether you have an unused ticket in your pocket.

Hours and frequency

The metro runs daily from 6am to 12:30am (midnight). Frequency varies by line and time of day: in central stations during peak hours, trains run every 2–4 minutes. At outer terminal stations on the M2, intervals can reach 30 minutes. On Saturday nights, selected lines operate until approximately 2am. On 25 December and 1 May, the metro runs from 7am to 7:30pm only.

The Milan metro for tourists

If you are staying in the historic centre around the Duomo, most major sights are within walking distance and you may not need the metro at all. The metro is most useful for:

Airports: M4 to Linate (San Babila → Linate, 15 min, €2.20) — Malpensa Express from Cadorna or Centrale (separate ticket, €13). Navigli district: M2 to Porta Genova. Brera gallery: M2 to Lanza or M3 to Montenapoleone. San Siro stadium: M5 to San Siro Stadio. Gae Aulenti / Porta Nuova: M2 or M5 to Garibaldi.

Frequently asked questions

How many metro lines does Milan have?+

Milan has 5 metro lines: M1 (red), M2 (green), M3 (yellow), M4 (blue — opened 2023) and M5 (purple). The network covers 94.5km with 113 stations — Italy's longest metro system.

What time does the Milan metro open and close?+

Daily from 6am to 12:30am. On 25 December and 1 May: 7am–7:30pm. On Saturday nights some lines run until ~2am.

How much is a Milan metro ticket?+

A single ATM ticket costs €2.20, valid 90 minutes on metro, tram and bus. 24-hour pass: €7.60. 48-hour pass: €14. Weekly: €17. Buy at station vending machines, newsagents or the ATM Milano app.

Which metro line goes to Linate Airport?+

Metro line M4 (blue line, opened 2023) connects Linate Airport to San Babila in approximately 15 minutes. Standard ATM ticket (€2.20). At San Babila, change to M1 for the Duomo and other central destinations.

Which metro line goes to Malpensa Airport?+

There is no direct metro to Malpensa. Take the Malpensa Express train from Milano Cadorna (M1/M2 station — 30 min, €13) or Milano Centrale (M2/M3 station — 50 min, €13). Trains every 20–30 min.

Where can I buy Milan metro tickets?+

At vending machines in all metro stations, at newsagents and tobacconists (tabaccherie) with the ATM logo, and via the ATM Milano app. Always validate before boarding — fines are €60–€100.
