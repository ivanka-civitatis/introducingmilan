Prices in Milan

Daily costs, restaurant prices, transport fares and budget guide for tourists

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Milan is one of the more expensive Italian cities — comparable to Rome and significantly pricier than Florence, Bologna or Naples. That said, it is possible to experience the city well on a moderate budget if you eat where locals eat and use public transport.

Daily budget estimates

💚 Budget — ~€80/day
Coffee at the bar, supermarket lunch, one museum visit (free days), public transport, dinner at a neighbourhood trattoria, house wine.
💛 Mid-range — ~€150/day
Sit-down café breakfast, 2 museum visits, aperitivo at a Brera bar, dinner at a proper restaurant with wine. No taxis.
❤️ Comfortable — €200+/day
Guided tours, quality restaurants, occasional taxis, shopping. Fashion Week and trade fair periods add significantly to hotel costs.
💡 Money-saving tips
Always drink coffee at the bar (not seated). Use free museum days (1st Sunday/3rd Tuesday). Eat lunch at a fixed-price trattoria. Avoid restaurants directly on the Duomo piazza.

Frequently asked questions

Is Milan expensive to visit?+

Milan is one of Italy's more expensive cities, but manageable on a moderate budget. A budget traveller spending ~€80/day can eat well (menù del giorno at a trattoria), visit free museums and use public transport comfortably. Hotel prices spike sharply during Fashion Week (February/September) and the Salone del Mobile in April.

How much does a meal cost in Milan?+

A set lunch menu (menù del giorno) at a neighbourhood trattoria costs €12–18 for two courses with wine. A mid-range dinner costs €25–45 per person. Avoid restaurants immediately around the Duomo — they charge a significant premium for average food.

Is coffee expensive in Milan?+

No — an espresso at the bar costs €1.20–1.50. The Italian habit is to stand at the bar and drink quickly. Sitting down at a table triggers a table supplement (coperto) that can double the price of your coffee.
