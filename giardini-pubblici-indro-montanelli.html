Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli

✓ Free · Open daily

The Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli is Milan's second largest park, covering 160,000 square metres in the elegant Porta Venezia district. Full of mature trees, fountains and green spaces, it is one of the most loved green areas in the city — and a perfect spot for a picnic, a morning jog or a relaxed afternoon with children.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo · Updated June 2026 · ⏱️ 3 min read

About the park

The Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli is located between Corso Venezia, Via Palestro, Via Manin and the Bastione di Porta Venezia — a prime position in one of Milan's most refined residential districts. The park was created in the late 19th century and is one of the oldest public gardens in the city.

In 2002 the park was renamed in honour of Indro Montanelli (1909–2001), one of Italy's most celebrated journalists and writers, who had a strong connection to Milan and spent many hours in these gardens. A statue of Montanelli reading a newspaper sits in the park as a tribute.

Things to do in the park

The park is more than just a green space — it contains several cultural attractions worth visiting during your stay:

🦕 Museo Civico di Storia Naturale
Milan's Natural History Museum, with collections dedicated to mineralogy, zoology and palaeontology. Located right inside the park grounds.
🖼️ Galleria d'Arte Moderna
The Modern Art Gallery is just 169 metres from the park. It houses an outstanding collection of 19th and 20th-century Italian and European art.
🏛️ Palazzo Dugnani
A remarkable 17th-century palace inside the park, featuring frescoes by Tiepolo. One of Milan's lesser-known architectural treasures.
🐴 Ponies & children's attractions
The park has ponies and other family-friendly attractions that make it a great destination for families with young children.

A green oasis in the city

Green spaces are precious in central Milan, and the Giardini Pubblici are one of the most refreshing and charming spots in the city. The mature trees provide excellent shade in summer, making it a far more comfortable afternoon option than the more exposed Parco Sempione. The park also features a scenic pond, particularly photogenic in spring and autumn.

The surrounding Porta Venezia neighbourhood is one of Milan's most cosmopolitan and lively districts — with excellent restaurants, bars and the famous Corso Buenos Aires shopping street just steps away.

Visiting tips

  • Combine with the nearby Galleria d'Arte Moderna — one of Milan's most underrated museums, just 169 metres from the park entrance.
  • The park is particularly beautiful in spring when the trees are in bloom.
  • If visiting with children, the ponies and playground area are in the southern section of the park.
  • The Corso Buenos Aires, one of Europe's longest shopping streets, starts right at Porta Venezia metro — ideal for combining a park visit with shopping.

Frequently asked questions

Are the Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli free?+

Yes, entry to the Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli is completely free. The park is open every day from 6:30am, closing at 9pm in winter and 11:30pm in summer.

What are the opening hours?+

October to April: 6:30am to 9pm. May: 6:30am to 10pm. June to September: 6:30am to 11:30pm. Open every day of the year.

What is there to do in Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli?+

The park contains the Natural History Museum of Milan, the 17th-century Palazzo Dugnani, and is right next to the Galleria d'Arte Moderna. There are also ponies and children's attractions. It is a popular spot for picnics, jogging and relaxing among locals.

How do I get to Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli?+

By metro: Palestro or Porta Venezia (line M1). By tram: lines 1, 2, 9, 29, 30 and 33 along Corso Venezia.
