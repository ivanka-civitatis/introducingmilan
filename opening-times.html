Opening Times in Milan

Museums, shops, restaurants and attractions — when does everything open?

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Museums and attractions

Most museums in Milan open Tuesday to Sunday from 10am to 6pm (or sometimes 5:30pm) and are closed on Mondays. The Last Supper (Cenacolo Vinciano) is closed on Mondays and requires advance booking. The Duomo and its complex are open daily but with varying hours.

🏛️ Most civic museums
Tuesday–Sunday: 10am–6pm (or 5:30pm). Closed Monday. Free on the first Sunday of the month and the third Tuesday after 2pm.
⛪ Churches
Generally open daily, but hours vary widely — typically 7am–12pm and 3pm–7pm. Many close during Sunday mass and on public holidays.
🎨 The Last Supper
Tuesday–Sunday: 8:15am–7pm. Closed Monday. Visits are timed and limited — advance booking essential.
⛪ Milan Cathedral (Duomo)
Daily from 8am (rooftop from 9am). Hours vary by season. The cathedral is free; rooftop and museum require tickets.

Shops

Most shops open Monday to Saturday from 10am to 7:30pm. Many shops in tourist and shopping areas also open on Sundays. Department stores and shopping centres typically stay open until 9pm and open seven days a week. Supermarkets generally open from 8am to 9pm, Monday to Saturday, with reduced hours on Sundays.

Restaurants & bars

Lunch is served approximately 12:30pm to 2:30pm. Many restaurants close in the afternoon and reopen for dinner. Dinner service typically begins 7:30–8pm, with kitchens closing around 10:30–11pm. Bars and cafés open from around 7am and often stay open late. Aperitivo is typically served from 6pm to 9pm.

Banks & public services

Banks are generally open Monday to Friday, 8:30am to 1:30pm, with some also opening in the afternoon (3–4:30pm). They are closed on weekends and public holidays. Post offices (Poste Italiane) are open Monday to Friday 8am to 6:30pm, and Saturday 8am to noon.

Free admission days

Milan's civic museums offer free entry on the first Sunday of every month — a national initiative that covers the Pinacoteca di Brera, the Galleria d'Arte Moderna, the Milan Archaeological Museum and other municipal museums. These days are popular and can be crowded; arriving at opening time is advisable.

The third Tuesday of the month from 2pm is a quieter free admission day at many civic museums — far less well-known than the Sunday option and consequently much less crowded. Excellent for visitors who want free entry without queues.

The Galleria d'Arte Moderna (GAM) is always free — no special days needed. So are the Cimitero Monumentale, the Basilica di Sant'Ambrogio courtyard, Piazza Mercanti and all of Milan's churches.

Opening times by category

State and civic museums (Brera, Ambrosiana, Archaeological Museum, GAM): typically Tuesday to Sunday, 10am to 6pm, closed Mondays. The Pinacoteca Ambrosiana is the exception — closed Wednesdays instead.

The Last Supper (Cenacolo Vinciano): Tuesday to Sunday, 8:15am to 7pm. Closed Mondays. Timed entry only — advance booking essential. Groups of 30 admitted every 15 minutes.

Milan Cathedral: open daily from 8am. Rooftop from 9am. Hours extend to 7pm in summer. The cathedral interior is free; roof and museum require tickets.

Castello Sforzesco: courtyards open daily 7am to 7:30pm (free). Museums inside Tuesday to Sunday 9am to 5:30pm.

Shops: Monday to Saturday 10am to 7:30pm. Many shops in tourist areas and shopping centres also open Sundays 10am–7pm or 10am–9pm.

Supermarkets: generally 8am to 9pm Monday to Saturday; reduced hours on Sundays (typically 9am to 8pm).

Pharmacies (farmacie): typically 8:30am to 7:30pm Monday to Friday, 9am to 1pm Saturday. A rota system ensures at least one pharmacy in each area is open evenings, Sundays and public holidays — the current duty pharmacy (farmacia di turno) is posted on all pharmacy windows.

Seasonal variations

Opening hours in Milan can vary significantly by season. Summer hours (June–September) are often extended, with monuments and attractions staying open until 7pm or later. Winter hours may be reduced. Some smaller museums and private venues close entirely for 2–3 weeks in August. Always verify opening hours directly with the attraction before visiting, particularly on or around public holidays.

Booking in advance

The following attractions should always be booked in advance to avoid disappointment: The Last Supper (often sold out 2–3 months ahead), the Milan Cathedral rooftop (can have long queues without advance booking), the Pinacoteca di Brera on the free admission Sunday. Guided tours for the Ambrosiana and Brera are also recommended to be booked ahead in high season.

Frequently asked questions

What days are museums free in Milan?+

The first Sunday of every month offers free entry to Milan's civic museums including the Pinacoteca di Brera, GAM and the Archaeological Museum. The third Tuesday of the month from 2pm is also a free entry day — quieter and less well-known. The Galleria d'Arte Moderna (GAM) is always free, every day, with no exceptions.

What time do shops open in Milan?+

Most shops in Milan open Monday to Saturday from 10am to 7:30pm. Many shops in tourist areas and shopping centres also open on Sundays. Supermarkets generally open from 8am to 9pm Monday to Saturday, with reduced Sunday hours. The Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II luxury boutiques keep longer hours.

What is closed on Monday in Milan?+

Most civic museums in Milan are closed on Mondays, including the Pinacoteca di Brera, the Galleria d'Arte Moderna and the Archaeological Museum. The Last Supper (Cenacolo Vinciano) is also closed on Mondays. The Pinacoteca Ambrosiana is the exception — it closes on Wednesdays. Churches, parks and monuments are generally open every day.

Do Milan attractions close in August?+

Major museums and monuments stay open in August, but some smaller galleries, restaurants and shops close for 1–3 weeks around Ferragosto (15 August). The city is noticeably quieter as many Milanese leave for their holidays — which can actually be a pleasant time to visit the main attractions with fewer crowds.
