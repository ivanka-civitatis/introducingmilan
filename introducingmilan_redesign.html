Are you planning to visit Milan? We can help you. At Introducing Milan you'll find updated information about what to see, where to stay, or the best places to eat.

Milan is internationally recognized as one of the world's most important fashion capitals, but it also has a wealth of interesting museums and things to see and do. In our Milan guide, you'll find all the necessary information to plan your stay in the capital of Lombardy and make the most of your holidays.

Tourism in Milan

Milan offers all the advantages of a large city, but it's relatively small, making it perfect to visit — tourists can get to most of the city's attractions on foot.

Some of the most beautiful attractions include the Duomo di Milano, an impressive Opera House, a striking castle, ancient churches, and a great variety of museums and art galleries.

For those who want to get away from the city, you can take the train to one of the prettiest landscapes of northern Italy, the Italian lakes. Its green hills and crystal-clear waters make it a perfect addition to any Milan trip.

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Milan

What are the top things to see in Milan? +

The unmissable highlights are the Duomo Cathedral and its rooftop terraces, Leonardo da Vinci's The Last Supper (book ahead!), the Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, Castello Sforzesco, and Teatro alla Scala. Art lovers should also visit the Pinacoteca di Brera.

How many days do you need in Milan? +

Two full days is enough to see the main attractions in the city centre. If you want to add a day trip to Lake Como or Lake Garda, plan for three to four days in total. A long weekend in Milan is very popular and very doable.

What are the best day trips from Milan? +

The most popular day trips from Milan are Lake Como (50 km, ~1 hour by train), Lake Garda, Bergamo, and Lake Maggiore. For something more adventurous, the Swiss Alps and the Bernina Express are reachable in about 2 hours.

Do I need to book The Last Supper in advance? +

Yes, absolutely. The Last Supper only admits small groups every 15 minutes and tickets sell out weeks — sometimes months — in advance. We strongly recommend booking before you travel. Our guided tour includes skip-the-line access.

What is the best time of year to visit Milan? +

Spring (April–June) and early autumn (September–October) are ideal, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds. July and August can be very hot and humid. Winter is cold but quieter, and the city is beautifully decorated for Christmas.
