Milan Malpensa Airport (MXP)

40 km from Milan city centre · Train 30–50 min · Private transfer available

Milan Malpensa Airport (MXP) is Milan's main international airport and Italy's second busiest, handling over 28 million passengers per year. It is located 40km (25 miles) northwest of the city centre in the province of Varese — further out than Linate, but well connected by train and bus.

The two terminals

Malpensa has two terminals located 4km apart. It is essential to know which terminal your flight uses before arriving. Both are connected by a free shuttle bus running every 7 minutes during the day (6am–11pm) and every 30 minutes at night.

Terminal 1
Most airlines
The main terminal — handles the vast majority of Malpensa's flights, including all long-haul routes and most European carriers. The Malpensa Express train departs from Terminal 1.
Terminal 2
Ryanair & low-cost
Almost exclusively used by Ryanair and a small number of other low-cost carriers. If flying with Ryanair, make sure you go to Terminal 2. No direct train — take the shuttle to T1 first.

How to get from Malpensa to Milan city centre

🚂
Malpensa Express — to Milano Centrale Recommended
Direct train from Terminal 1 to Milano Centrale — the main railway station. Journey time: ~50 minutes. Price: €13 one way. Trains run every 20–30 minutes. The most convenient option if you are arriving at Terminal 1 and heading to a hotel near the centre or Centrale.
🚂
Malpensa Express — to Milano Cadorna
Direct train from Terminal 1 to Milano Cadorna-Castello — closest to Castello Sforzesco and Parco Sempione. Journey time: ~30 minutes. Price: €13 one way. Trains run every 30 minutes. The fastest option into the city — choose this if your hotel is in the western or northern centre.
🚌
Bus (Terravision / Autostradale) Cheapest
Coach services run between Malpensa and Milano Centrale. Journey time: ~50 minutes (can be longer in traffic). Price: €10 one way (Terravision). Runs approximately every 30 minutes. Good value but less reliable in traffic — allow extra time if you have a connection.
🚕
Taxi — fixed price
Official taxis from Malpensa to any address in central Milan operate at a fixed price of €100. No surcharges should be applied. Journey time: approximately 50 minutes in normal traffic, longer during rush hour. Only use official white taxis from the designated rank outside arrivals.

From Malpensa to Lake Maggiore

One of the advantages of landing at Malpensa is its proximity to Lake Maggiore — approximately 37km from the airport. Many travellers choose to visit the lake directly from the airport before continuing to Milan. This can be arranged via private transfer or by car rental. Lake Maggiore day trip guide →

Useful tips

Always check which terminal your flight uses — Terminal 1 and Terminal 2 are not adjacent and the shuttle journey adds time. If flying with Ryanair, you will almost certainly depart from or arrive at Terminal 2. To take the Malpensa Express train, walk through Terminal 1 to the station below — signs are clearly marked. At Terminal 2, take the shuttle to Terminal 1 and then the train.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Malpensa Airport to Milan city centre?+

Four options: (1) Malpensa Express train to Centrale (50 min, €13); (2) Malpensa Express to Cadorna (30 min, €13); (3) Bus to Centrale (50 min, €10); (4) Taxi (fixed €100, ~50 min); (5) Private transfer, booked in advance, door to hotel.

What is the difference between Malpensa Terminal 1 and Terminal 2?+

Terminal 1 handles the vast majority of airlines. Terminal 2 is used almost exclusively by Ryanair and a few other low-cost carriers. They are 4km apart and connected by a free shuttle bus every 7 minutes during the day.

How far is Malpensa from Milan?+

Malpensa is approximately 40km (25 miles) northwest of Milan city centre. By train the journey takes 30–50 minutes; by taxi approximately 50 minutes in normal traffic.

Is there a direct train from Malpensa to Milan Centrale?+

Yes — the Malpensa Express runs directly from Terminal 1 to Milano Centrale in approximately 50 minutes (€13 one way). There is also a service to Milano Cadorna in 30 minutes (€13). Trains run every 20–30 minutes.

How much is a taxi from Malpensa to Milan?+

The taxi from Malpensa to Milan city centre has a fixed price of €100. No surcharges should be applied. The journey takes approximately 50 minutes in normal traffic.

Can I go to Lake Maggiore directly from Malpensa?+

Yes — Lake Maggiore is approximately 37km from Malpensa Airport and can be reached directly by private transfer or car. This makes Malpensa a convenient starting point for visiting the lake without first going into Milan.
