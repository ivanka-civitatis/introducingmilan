Milan Linate Airport (LIN)

Just 8km from the city centre · Metro M4 in 15 minutes · The most convenient airport for Milan

Milan Linate Airport (LIN), officially named Aeroporto Enrico Forlanini, is the closest airport to Milan city centre — just 8km southeast. Since the opening of Metro line M4 in 2023, getting from Linate to the centre takes just 15 minutes for €2.20, making it by far the most convenient of Milan's three airports when available.

How to get from Linate Airport to Milan city centre

🚇
Metro M4 (Blue Line) Recommended since 2023
~15 min · €2.20 · valid 90 min
Since its opening in 2023, Metro line M4 connects Linate Airport directly to San Babila station in approximately 15 minutes. From San Babila, change to Metro M1 (red line) to reach Duomo (1 stop), Cadorna (4 stops) or any other city destination. A single ATM ticket costs €2.20 and is valid for 90 minutes. This is now the fastest, cheapest and most straightforward option from Linate.
🚌
Bus 73 / X73 Cheapest
San Babila metro · every 10 min · city ticket
The urban bus lines 73 (daily, every 10 min) and X73 (Mon–Fri, every 20 min) connect Linate to San Babila metro station. First bus at 6:05am, last at 12:40am. Uses the standard ATM city ticket (€2.20). Note: buying the ticket on the bus incurs a supplement — buy in advance at the airport or via the ATM app.
🚌
Starfly / Air Bus — to Milano Centrale
~25 min · €5 one way · €9 return
Starfly runs to Lambrate and Milano Centrale stations every 20–30 minutes, 6:10am–11:30pm (Mon–Fri), 6:30am–10pm (weekends). The Air Bus runs to Milano Centrale (Piazza Luigi da Savoia) and Piazza Dateo, 6:30am–11:30pm, 25 minutes. Both cost €5 one way or €9 return. A good option if you need to reach Centrale directly with luggage.
🚕
Taxi — metered fare
~20 min · €30–€40 (no fixed price)
Unlike Malpensa, Linate taxis do not have a fixed price — they charge by the meter. Expect to pay €30–€40 to the city centre depending on traffic and exact destination. Taxis are available from the designated rank outside arrivals. Only use official white taxis.

About Linate Airport

Linate is Milan's second airport by passenger traffic — smaller and quieter than Malpensa, handling around 9.5 million passengers per year. It deals exclusively with European and domestic routes; if you are flying long-haul, your flight will arrive at Malpensa. Airlines that regularly fly to Linate include ITA Airways (former Alitalia), British Airways, Lufthansa, Air France and other European carriers.

The airport is compact and easy to navigate — a welcome contrast to Malpensa. Arrivals, departures, ground transport and car rental are all on a single level, and the walk from the gate to the exit is typically under 15 minutes.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Linate Airport to Milan city centre?+

The best option since 2023 is Metro M4 to San Babila (~15 min, €2.20), then change to M1. Alternatively: Bus 73 to San Babila (every 10 min), Starfly/Air Bus to Centrale (25 min, €5), taxi (€30–€40 metered) or private transfer.

Is there a metro from Linate Airport to Milan?+

Yes — since 2023, Metro line M4 connects Linate to San Babila in approximately 15 minutes (€2.20). At San Babila, change to Metro M1 for the Duomo, Cadorna or other destinations.

How far is Linate from Milan city centre?+

Linate Airport is approximately 8km (5 miles) southeast of Milan city centre — the closest of Milan's three airports. Metro M4 takes about 15 minutes; taxi approximately 20 minutes.

How much is a taxi from Linate to Milan?+

Linate taxis charge by the meter — there is no fixed price. Expect to pay €30–€40 to the city centre depending on traffic and exact destination.

Which airlines fly to Milan Linate?+

Linate handles European and domestic routes only. Airlines include ITA Airways, British Airways, Lufthansa, Air France and other European carriers. No long-haul flights operate from Linate — those arrive at Malpensa.
