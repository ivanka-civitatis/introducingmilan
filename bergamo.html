Bergamo

One of northern Italy's finest medieval cities — Città Alta, Venetian walls & breathtaking views. 60km from Milan.

Bergamo is one of the finest and most rewarding day trips from Milan — a beautifully preserved walled city just 60km away. Its Città Alta (Upper City) is one of the best medieval city centres in northern Italy, enclosed by 16th-century Venetian walls and reached by a charming funicular from the modern lower town.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026·⏱️ 3 min read

Città Alta vs Città Bassa

Bergamo is divided into two distinct parts. The Città Alta (Upper City) is the historic heart — perched on a hill, surrounded by UNESCO-listed Venetian walls, and full of medieval and Renaissance buildings, narrow cobbled streets and excellent restaurants. This is where visitors should spend their time. The Città Bassa (Lower City) is the modern, commercial part of Bergamo, which is less interesting for day-trippers. The two are connected by a short funicular railway.

Things to see in the Città Alta

🏛️ Piazza Vecchia
The magnificent main square of the upper city — the political and administrative heart of medieval Bergamo. Surrounded by the Palazzo della Ragione, Palazzo del Comune, Palazzo dei Podestà and the Torre Civica. One of the finest squares in Italy.
🗼 Torre Civica
The 52-metre civic tower, symbol of medieval Bergamo. Climb to the top for panoramic views over the upper city, the lower town and, on clear days, the Lombard plain stretching to the Alps.
⛪ Basilica Santa Maria Maggiore
A monumental Romanesque basilica on Piazza Duomo, rivalling the nearby cathedral in scale and beauty. The Colleoni Chapel attached to it — the funerary chapel of the condottiere Bartolomeo Colleoni — is one of the finest examples of early Renaissance architecture in Lombardy.
🏰 Venetian walls
The UNESCO World Heritage defensive walls built by the Venetian Republic in the 16th century — nearly 6km in circumference with four historic gates. Walking along the ramparts offers spectacular views over the surrounding landscape.
⛪ Bergamo Cathedral
The Cathedral of Sant'Alessandro, dedicated to Bergamo's patron saint, stands on Piazza Duomo alongside Santa Maria Maggiore. Its Baroque interior contains paintings by Tiepolo.
🚡 The funicular
The historic funicular railway connecting Città Bassa to Città Alta is itself a small pleasure — a short climb through the hillside with views over the lower city. A second funicular continues from the upper town to San Vigilio, with even more panoramic views.
🎵 Gaetano Donizetti

Bergamo is the birthplace of Gaetano Donizetti (1797–1848), one of the great composers of Italian opera — creator of L'elisir d'amore, Lucia di Lammermoor and Don Pasquale. The Donizetti Museum is in the upper city, and the Teatro Donizetti in the lower town hosts an annual opera festival in his honour. The connection with opera is deep — Bergamo's wealthy families were major patrons of the art form.

Visiting tips

  • Focus your visit on the Città Alta — the lower city is pleasant but ordinary. The upper city alone deserves at least 3–4 hours.
  • Take the funicular up from Piazza Mercato delle Scarpe — it's a delight and saves a steep climb.
  • The Torre Civica has a small entrance fee but the views are exceptional — worth the climb.
  • Bergamo is famous for polenta and casoncelli (a local stuffed pasta) — look for restaurants in the upper city's side streets rather than on Piazza Vecchia itself.
  • If visiting by car, consider combining with Lake Iseo (31km away) for a full day in Bergamo province.
  • Bergamo Airport (Orio al Serio, BGY) is 4km from the city centre — an excellent option if flying into or out of this airport with Ryanair or other low-cost carriers.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Milan to Bergamo?+

By train: Milano Centrale or Porta Garibaldi → Bergamo, less than 1 hour, ~€6 one way. By car: A4 motorway east, ~60km, ~40–45 minutes. Bergamo also has its own airport (Orio al Serio), 4km from the city centre.

What is the Città Alta in Bergamo?+

The Città Alta (Upper City) is the historic medieval centre of Bergamo, perched on a hill and surrounded by 16th-century UNESCO-listed Venetian walls. It is one of the best-preserved medieval city centres in northern Italy. Access by funicular from the lower town or on foot through one of four historic gates.

Is Bergamo worth a day trip from Milan?+

Absolutely — Bergamo is one of the finest day trips from Milan. The Città Alta is visually exceptional, the food is outstanding, and the contrast with Milan's modernity makes it feel like a completely different world despite being just an hour away.

How far is Bergamo from Milan?+

Bergamo is approximately 60km northeast of Milan — about 40–45 minutes by car on the A4 motorway, or less than 1 hour by train.
