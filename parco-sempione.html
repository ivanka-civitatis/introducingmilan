Parco Sempione

✓ Free · Open daily from 6am

Parco Sempione is the green lung of Milan — the city's largest central park, located right next to Castello Sforzesco. Built between 1890 and 1893 in the style of a traditional English garden, it is a favourite escape for locals and a perfect afternoon destination for visitors.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo · Updated June 2026 · ⏱️ 4 min read

About the park

Parco Sempione occupies the former parade grounds of Castello Sforzesco, which were transformed into a public park in the late 19th century. The landscape design was the work of Emilio Alemagna, who modelled it on the English garden tradition — winding paths, a lake, rolling lawns and mature trees rather than the formal geometric layouts typical of Italian parks of the period.

Today it covers approximately 386,000 square metres, making it by far the largest green space in central Milan. On any given weekend, you'll find Milanese jogging, picnicking, reading, and simply unwinding — it is one of the most genuinely local experiences the city offers.

Things to do in Parco Sempione

While a simple stroll through the park is rewarding in itself, Parco Sempione is home to several remarkable attractions worth including in your visit:

🏰 Castello Sforzesco
The 14th-century fortress at the park's edge houses 7 museums, including Michelangelo's Rondanini Pietà. The courtyard is free; museums from €10.
🏛️ Triennale di Milano
A celebrated museum dedicated to contemporary design, fine arts and Italian avant-garde architecture. One of Europe's most respected design institutions.
🐟 Acquario Civico Milano
The third-oldest aquarium in Europe, housed in a beautiful Art Nouveau building. Family-friendly and explorable in under an hour — perfect as an add-on to your park visit.
🗼 Branca Tower
An iconic observation tower with panoramic views over the park, the city and, on a clear day, the Alps. Well worth the short climb.
🏛️ Arco della Pace
A monumental triumphal arch at the northern entrance to the park. Built in 1807 and completed in 1838 — one of the most photographed spots in Milan at sunset.

The Arco della Pace

Standing at the northern end of the park on Corso Sempione, the Arco della Pace (Arch of Peace) is one of Milan's most impressive monuments. It was commissioned by Napoleon in 1807 as a triumphal arch and completed in 1838 under Austrian rule — by which point its dedication had been changed to celebrate the peace following the Congress of Vienna.

The arch is adorned with bronze reliefs and topped by a Sextiga (a chariot drawn by six horses) representing the Goddess of Peace. It is one of the most photographed monuments in Milan, particularly at sunset when the light is spectacular.

Visiting tips

  • The park is spacious and summer shade is limited — bring water and sunscreen if visiting in July or August.
  • Plan at least 2–3 hours to enjoy the park fully, or a full day if you want to visit the museums and landmarks inside.
  • The lake area is the most scenic part of the park — ideal for picnics and photos.
  • Visit the Arco della Pace at sunset for the best light and the most atmospheric photographs.
  • Combine with a visit to nearby Castello Sforzesco — the castle is right at the park's edge and the courtyard is free.

Frequently asked questions

Is Parco Sempione free?+

Yes, Parco Sempione is completely free to enter. It is open daily from 6am to midnight in summer (April–September) and from 6am to 10pm in winter (October–March).

What are the opening hours of Parco Sempione?+

April to September: daily from 6am to midnight. October to March: daily from 6am to 10pm. The park is open every day of the year.

What is there to do in Parco Sempione?+

Parco Sempione is home to Castello Sforzesco, the Triennale di Milano design museum, the Acquario Civico (third-oldest aquarium in Europe), the Branca Tower with panoramic views, and the Arco della Pace triumphal arch. It is also a popular spot for jogging, picnicking and relaxing.

What is the Arco della Pace?+

The Arco della Pace (Arch of Peace) is a monumental triumphal arch at the northern end of Parco Sempione. Built in 1807 and completed in 1838, it is one of the most photographed monuments in Milan, especially at sunset.

How do I get to Parco Sempione?+

By metro: Cadorna or Cairoli (line M1), or Lanza (line M2). By tram: lines 1, 2, 4, 12, 14 and 19. By bus: lines 18, 37, 50, 58, 61 and 94.
