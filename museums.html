Museums in Milan

Art galleries, science museums, house museums and free entries — the complete guide

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Milan has an exceptional concentration of world-class museums for a city of its size. The Pinacoteca di Brera is one of Italy's finest art galleries; the Pinacoteca Ambrosiana holds Leonardo's Codex Atlanticus and Caravaggio's first still life; the Galleria d'Arte Moderna gives you Van Gogh and Cézanne free of charge. And most visitors only discover half of them.

Art galleries

Milan's art galleries form one of the richest concentrations of Italian Renaissance and European modern painting in the country — spread across a compact historic centre. The Brera and the Ambrosiana are the two most important; the Poldi Pezzoli and the GAM are the most underrated.

Design, science & speciality museums

Beyond the art galleries, Milan has a remarkable range of speciality museums. The Science Museum is Italy's largest and excellent for families; the Triennale is essential for anyone interested in design and architecture; the Archaeological Museum brings Roman Mediolanum to life with visible city walls from the 3rd century AD.

Free museums in Milan

Milan has more free museum options than most visitors realise. The Galleria d'Arte Moderna is always free — no exceptions, no special days needed. The Cimitero Monumentale functions as an open-air sculpture museum of extraordinary quality. And the civic museums offer free entry on the first Sunday and third Tuesday of every month.

Useful visiting tips

Most civic museums in Milan are closed on Mondays (the Ambrosiana on Wednesdays). The first Sunday of the month brings free entry to many civic museums — popular but crowded. The third Tuesday of the month after 2pm is also free and much quieter. A guided tour is strongly recommended for the Brera and the Ambrosiana — the collections reward context.

Frequently asked questions

Which is the best museum in Milan?+

The Pinacoteca di Brera is Milan's finest art gallery — one of the best in Italy — with Raphael, Mantegna, Tintoretto and Hayez. For a free option, the Galleria d'Arte Moderna has Van Gogh, Cézanne and Picasso at no charge. The Pinacoteca Ambrosiana is essential for Leonardo da Vinci and Caravaggio.

Which museums in Milan are free?+

The Galleria d'Arte Moderna (GAM) is always free. The Cimitero Monumentale is also free. Civic museums offer free entry on the first Sunday and third Tuesday (after 2pm) of each month. The Poldi Pezzoli and the Brera offer free or reduced entry for under-18s.

Are Milan museums closed on Mondays?+

Most municipal museums in Milan are closed on Mondays — including the GAM, Pinacoteca di Brera and Archaeological Museum. The Pinacoteca Ambrosiana is closed on Wednesdays instead. Always check individual opening hours before visiting.

Is the Pinacoteca di Brera worth visiting?+

Yes — the Brera is one of Italy's finest art galleries and, because it is in Milan rather than Florence or Rome, significantly less crowded than it deserves to be. A guided tour is strongly recommended. Allow 2–3 hours minimum.
