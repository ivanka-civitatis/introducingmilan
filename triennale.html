Triennale di Milano

The Triennale di Milano is one of Europe's most respected institutions dedicated to design and architecture — and the only museum in the world devoted entirely to Italian design. Founded in 1923, it is housed in the elegant Palazzo dell'Arte in Parco Sempione, next to Castello Sforzesco.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026·⏱️ 3 min read

About the Triennale

The Triennale di Milano was founded in 1923 with the mission to promote Italian fine arts, decorative arts and architecture. Its home, the Palazzo dell'Arte, was built in 1933 as the permanent headquarters for the International Exhibition of Decorative Arts — itself originally a triennial event, hence the name.

Today the Triennale functions as both a permanent Design Museum and an active cultural venue with a year-round programme of temporary exhibitions dedicated to contemporary design, architecture, fashion and visual culture. Its position — in the southeast corner of Parco Sempione, 190 metres from Castello Sforzesco — makes it a natural complement to any visit to the park or the castle.

What to see

The permanent Design Museum presents a vast survey of Italian design through a permanent collection — furniture, objects, graphics and industrial design spanning the 20th century to the present, displayed in a large central hall.

The remaining rooms host temporary exhibitions that change every few months, covering avant-garde design, architecture, fashion, photography and Italian cultural history. The quality and scope of exhibitions varies significantly — if you are interested in a specific theme, it is worth checking the current programme on the Triennale website before visiting.

The museum café and bookshop are well worth visiting in their own right — the bookshop is one of the best design and architecture bookshops in Italy.

Is the Triennale worth visiting?

The Triennale is best suited for visitors with a specific interest in design, architecture or contemporary Italian culture. Because the temporary exhibitions change every few months, it is impossible to predict what will be on during your visit. The permanent collection alone may not justify the ticket price for general visitors — but for design enthusiasts, a visit to the Triennale is unmissable.

If you are visiting Parco Sempione or Castello Sforzesco, it is worth checking the current programme in advance. The bookshop and café are worth a visit regardless of the exhibitions.

Visiting tips

  • Check the current exhibitions on the Triennale website before visiting — the programme changes significantly throughout the year.
  • Ticket prices vary depending on the exhibitions — confirm the current price on the website.
  • Combine with a visit to Parco Sempione (190 metres) and Castello Sforzesco (501 metres) for a full morning in this part of the city.
  • The bookshop has one of the best collections of design and architecture books in Italy — worth browsing even if you skip the exhibitions.
  • The museum café has an outdoor terrace facing the park — a pleasant spot for a break.

Frequently asked questions

What is the Triennale di Milano?+

The Triennale di Milano is an international cultural institution founded in 1923, dedicated to design, architecture and the Italian avant-garde. It includes a permanent Design Museum and changing temporary exhibitions. It is housed in the Palazzo dell'Arte in Parco Sempione.

What are the opening hours of the Triennale?+

Tuesday to Sunday from 10:30am to 8pm. Closed on Mondays.

How much does it cost to visit?+

Ticket prices vary depending on the exhibitions currently on show. Check the Triennale website before visiting to confirm current prices.

How do I get to the Triennale di Milano?+

By metro: Cadorna (lines M1 and M2), then walk through the park. By bus: line 61 (Triennale stop). The museum is in the Palazzo dell'Arte in Parco Sempione, at Viale Alemagna, 6.
