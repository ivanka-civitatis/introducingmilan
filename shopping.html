Shopping in Milan

Luxury fashion, high street, vintage markets & outlets — for every budget

Milan is one of the world's great shopping cities — not just for luxury fashion, but for all levels of the market. From the Quadrilatero della Moda (the luxury fashion quarter on Via Montenapoleone) to the vintage stalls of the Navigli and the outlet malls outside the city, there is something for every budget and taste.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Quadrilatero della Moda — the luxury district

The Quadrilatero della Moda (Fashion Quarter) is formed by four streets in the Brera/Montenapoleone area: Via Montenapoleone, Via della Spiga, Via Sant'Andrea and Corso Venezia. These streets house the flagships of virtually every major luxury brand — Gucci, Prada, Versace, Dolce & Gabbana, Armani, Valentino, Hermès, Louis Vuitton, Chanel and more. Even if you are not buying, it is worth walking through for the window displays and the extraordinary concentration of beautiful buildings.

Metro: M3 Montenapoleone — the station is named after the street.

Corso Buenos Aires — high street shopping

Corso Buenos Aires (near Porta Venezia) is Milan's longest and busiest shopping street — over 1.5km of mid-range and high-street brands including Zara, H&M, Pull&Bear, Bershka and dozens of Italian chains. It runs from Porta Venezia to Piazzale Loreto and is most easily accessed by metro M1 (Lima or Loreto stops).

Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II

The Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II — a spectacular 19th-century arcade connecting the Duomo with La Scala — houses high-end stores including Prada, Gucci, Louis Vuitton and Versace Home, as well as some of Milan's most historic cafés. Even for non-shoppers, it is one of the unmissable sights in the city.

Navigli — vintage & independent

The Navigli canal district is the best area for vintage clothing, independent boutiques, design shops and antique dealers. The Mercatone dell'Antiquariato — a large antique market held on the last Sunday of the month along the Naviglio Grande — is one of the best antique markets in northern Italy.

Outlets near Milan

🇨🇭 Foxtown — Mendrisio (40km)
In Switzerland, just over the border. Large outlet with luxury Italian and international brands at significant discounts. Check VAT refund rules for non-EU purchases.
🛍️ Serravalle Outlet (90km)
One of Italy's largest outlet malls, near Alessandria. Over 300 brands including Gucci, Prada, Burberry, Nike and more. Accessible by shuttle from Milan Centrale.
🛍️ Vicolungo Outlets (60km)
Near Novara — a smaller, more manageable outlet with Italian and European brands. Accessible by car from the A4 motorway.
🛍️ Fidenza Village (110km)
Premium open-air outlet village near Parma — Italian food and fashion brands in a pleasant setting. Best by car or organised shuttle.

Opening hours

Most shops in Milan open Monday to Saturday 10am–7:30pm. Many shops now also open on Sundays, especially in tourist areas and shopping centres. The Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II is open daily. Department stores and shopping centres have longer hours, typically 10am–9pm.

During the January and July/August sales (saldi), discounts in Italian shops can reach 50–70% — a good time to visit if shopping is a priority.

Frequently asked questions

What is the fashion district in Milan?+

The Quadrilatero della Moda — formed by Via Montenapoleone, Via della Spiga, Via Sant'Andrea and Corso Venezia. Flagship stores of Gucci, Prada, Versace, Armani and all major luxury brands. Metro: M3 Montenapoleone.

Where can I shop for affordable fashion in Milan?+

Corso Buenos Aires (Porta Venezia, M1 Lima/Loreto) is Milan's longest high-street shopping strip with Zara, H&M and Italian mid-range chains. The Navigli is best for vintage and independent boutiques.

Are there outlet malls near Milan?+

Yes: Foxtown (40km, Switzerland), Serravalle Outlet (90km, 300+ brands), Vicolungo (60km) and Fidenza Village (110km). Serravalle has shuttle buses from Milan Centrale.
