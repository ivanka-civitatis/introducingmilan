Lake Garda

Italy's largest lake — Sirmione, Gardaland & the Malcesine funicular. 140km from Milan.

Lake Garda (Lago di Garda) is Italy's largest lake and the most lively — a broad expanse of crystal-clear water ringed by charming towns, medieval castles and, to the north, dramatic Alpine peaks. Unlike the quieter Lake Como or Lake Maggiore, Garda buzzes with energy throughout the summer season.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026·⏱️ 3 min read

What to see

🏰 Sirmione
The most famous stop — a narrow peninsula on the southern shore crowned by the striking Rocca Scaligera medieval fortress. Nearby, the Grotte di Catullo offers remains of an ancient Roman villa. Beautiful, but very crowded in high season — visit early morning if possible.
🎢 Gardaland
Italy's most visited amusement park, on the southeastern shore. An excellent choice for families with children, with a wide range of rides for all ages. Book in advance in summer.
🚡 Malcesine & Monte Baldo
A beautiful medieval village on the eastern shore, with a funicular to Monte Baldo (1,748m) — spectacular panoramic views of the lake and the Alps. One of the most memorable experiences on the lake.
🌊 Other villages
Riva del Garda (north, Alpine atmosphere), Desenzano (lively southern town, good for swimming), Peschiera del Garda (fortified Venetian walls) and Salò (elegant lakeside promenade).

Best time to visit

May and September are the ideal months — warm enough to enjoy the lake fully, with far fewer tourists and no parking nightmare. June to August offers the best swimming weather but maximum crowds, especially in Sirmione. Winter is quiet and many businesses close.

Visiting tips

  • Sirmione is very popular — arrive early (before 10am) in summer to avoid the worst of the crowds and find parking.
  • The Malcesine–Monte Baldo funicular is one of the best experiences on the lake — book the cable car in advance in July and August.
  • Gardaland is excellent for families; book tickets online to skip queues at the gate.
  • Car ferries cross the lake between Torri del Benaco and Toscolano-Maderno — useful if you want to explore both shores.
  • Combine with a visit to Verona (30 minutes from Sirmione by car) for a full day in the Veneto.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Milan to Lake Garda?+

By car: motorway direction Verona — 140km, ~1.5 hours. By train: Milano Centrale to Desenzano del Garda-Sirmione, 50 min–1.5h, €11–€31. By guided tour: several day trips from Milan include Sirmione and Verona.

Is Sirmione worth visiting?+

Yes, but be prepared for crowds. The Rocca Scaligera castle and the Grotte di Catullo Roman ruins are genuinely impressive. Visit early in the morning or in shoulder season (May or September) for a more enjoyable experience.

What is the best time to visit Lake Garda?+

May and September are the best months — warm weather, far fewer crowds, and the lake is still beautiful. June to August offers swimming weather but maximum crowds and parking difficulties.

How far is Lake Garda from Milan?+

Lake Garda is approximately 140km east of Milan — about 1.5 hours by car on the motorway, or 50 minutes to 1.5 hours by train to Desenzano del Garda-Sirmione station.
