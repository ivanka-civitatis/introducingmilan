Piazza Gae Aulenti

✓ Free · Open 24h

Piazza Gae Aulenti is the epicentre of Milan's most modern neighbourhood — the Porta Nuova district. Inaugurated on 8 December 2012 and dedicated to the renowned Italian architect Gae Aulenti, the square is surrounded by some of the most striking contemporary buildings in Italy, including the country's tallest skyscraper.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo · Updated June 2026 · ⏱️ 4 min read

The square and the Porta Nuova district

The Piazza Gae Aulenti and the surrounding buildings are the result of one of the most ambitious urban regeneration projects in Italy — the redevelopment of the Garibaldi, Isola and Varesine neighbourhoods into a modern financial and residential district. The masterplan was directed by the Argentine architect César Pelli.

The square itself is elevated 6 metres above street level, connected to the surrounding streets by ramps, escalators and bridges. The result is a surprisingly intimate public space in the middle of an otherwise dramatic architectural landscape.

The most impressive buildings

The square is surrounded by a series of architecturally striking buildings, each worth seeing in its own right:

🏢 Unicredit Tower
At 231 metres, this is currently the tallest skyscraper in Italy. Designed by César Pelli, its distinctive spire dominates the Milan skyline. The tower houses the headquarters of UniCredit bank.
🌿 Bosco Verticale (Vertical Forest)
A pair of residential towers covered by over 2,000 species of plants and trees. Completed in 2014 and designed by Stefano Boeri, it has won multiple international architecture awards and is one of the most photographed buildings in Milan.
🌳 The Solar Tree
At the centre of the square, the Solar Tree lights the entire area with LED lighting powered by solar energy — a symbol of the district's commitment to sustainability.
🌉 Corso Como Footbridge
A pedestrian footbridge connects the square to Corso Como, one of Milan's liveliest streets with boutiques, restaurants and bars.

Aperitivo and nightlife

Piazza Gae Aulenti is one of the best areas in Milan for going out. Along with Navigli, it is the most popular destination for the Milanese aperitivo tradition — from around 6pm to 9pm, the bars around the square fill up with locals enjoying Campari spritz, Negroni and complementary snacks.

After dinner, the area transforms into one of the city's liveliest nightlife spots, with cocktail bars, clubs and late-night restaurants open well past midnight.

When the sun sets

At night, Piazza Gae Aulenti truly comes alive. The square hosts a light, music and water show after sunset, making it one of the most atmospheric spots in Milan in the evening. The illuminated towers and the Solar Tree create a striking visual contrast with the historic city centre just a few kilometres away.

Visiting tips

  • Visit at dusk for the best combination of natural light and the illuminated buildings — the Bosco Verticale at sunset is spectacular.
  • Walk along Corso Como after visiting the square — it is one of Milan's most fashionable streets, packed with boutiques, galleries and restaurants.
  • The area is very close to the Cimitero Monumentale — combine both for a fascinating contrast between Milan's historic past and its ultramodern present.
  • The Garibaldi FS metro station gives direct access to both lines M2 and M5 — very well connected to the rest of the city.

Frequently asked questions

What is Piazza Gae Aulenti?+

Piazza Gae Aulenti is the centrepiece of Milan's modern Porta Nuova district, inaugurated on 8 December 2012. It is surrounded by striking contemporary buildings including the Unicredit Tower (Italy's tallest skyscraper at 231 metres) and the Bosco Verticale (Vertical Forest).

Is Piazza Gae Aulenti free to visit?+

Yes, Piazza Gae Aulenti is a public square, completely free to visit and open 24 hours a day. Individual shops, restaurants and bars have their own opening hours.

What is the Bosco Verticale?+

The Bosco Verticale (Vertical Forest) is a pair of residential towers near Piazza Gae Aulenti covered by over 2,000 species of plants and trees. Completed in 2014 and designed by Stefano Boeri, it has won multiple international architecture awards and is one of the most photographed buildings in Milan.

Is Piazza Gae Aulenti good for nightlife?+

Yes, along with Navigli, Piazza Gae Aulenti is one of the best areas in Milan for going out. It is particularly lively during the aperitivo hour (6–9pm) and offers countless clubs, restaurants and trendy bars open well past midnight.

How do I get to Piazza Gae Aulenti?+

By metro: Garibaldi FS (lines M2 and M5) — the station gives direct access to the square. By tram: Stazione Garibaldi (lines 10 and 33).
