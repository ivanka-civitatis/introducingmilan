Lake Iseo

Lombardy's quietest lake — Monte Isola, tranquil villages and no crowds. 90km from Milan.

Lake Iseo (Lago d'Iseo) is the most overlooked of Lombardy's major lakes — smaller and far less visited than Como or Garda, but no less beautiful. Straddling the provinces of Bergamo and Brescia, it offers tranquil villages, dramatic scenery and the remarkable Monte Isola — the largest lake island in Southern Europe.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026·⏱️ 3 min read

About Lake Iseo

Lake Iseo is known in Italy as a local favourite — a place where Lombard families come on summer weekends for peace and genuine lakeside life, rather than a tourist destination in the traditional sense. This makes it ideal for visitors who want to experience something more authentic than the more famous northern lakes, with landscapes that are every bit as beautiful.

The lake became briefly world-famous in 2016 when the artist Christo installed The Floating Piers — a walkway of saffron-coloured fabric connecting Monte Isola to the shore — which attracted over 1.2 million visitors in just 16 days. Though the installation is gone, the memory of it drives continued interest in the lake.

What to see

🏝️ Monte Isola
The highlight of the lake — a car-free island with a surface of 4.5 km², a 14th-century castle (Rocca Martinengo), and the sanctuary of the Madonna della Ceriola at its summit (600m). Reached by ferry from Sulzano or Sale Marasino. Peaceful, lush and utterly charming.
🏘️ Iseo
The main town on the lake — a charming medieval centre with porticoed buildings, cobbled streets and a good waterfront. The best starting point for a day trip and the hub for ferry connections to Monte Isola.
⛰️ Pisogne
A pretty commune on the northern shore where the mountains descend dramatically to the water's edge. The fresco cycle in the Santa Maria della Neve church has earned it the nickname "the poor man's Sistine Chapel".
🌊 Sarnico
An attractive town on the southern end of the lake, separated from Paratico by a bridge over the Oglio river. Excellent for cycling along the lakeside and a pleasant base for exploring the southern shore.
🚂 Heritage railway

Lake Iseo can be explored by a historic steam-powered heritage railway that runs along the shore on a handful of days each year — one of the most atmospheric ways to experience the lake. Check timetables at ferrovieturistiche.it to see if a train runs during your visit.

Visiting tips

  • The car is the most flexible option — it allows you to combine Lake Iseo with nearby Bergamo (31 km) in a single day.
  • From Iseo town, take the ferry to Monte Isola (departures from Sulzano and Sale Marasino) — the crossing takes about 10 minutes.
  • Monte Isola is car-free — bikes can be hired on the island for exploring.
  • The church of Santa Maria della Neve in Pisogne is worth the detour for the extraordinary frescoes — often closed, so check locally before visiting.
  • Lake Iseo is best visited in spring or early autumn — summer weekends can be busy with Italian day-trippers, though nothing like the crowds at Como or Garda.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Milan to Lake Iseo?+

By car: motorway direction Verona, exit at Iseo — ~90km, ~1 hour. By train: Milano Centrale to Brescia (35–90 min, €8–€27), then change for Iseo (30 min, €3.60). Car is recommended for flexibility.

What is Monte Isola?+

Monte Isola is an island in the middle of Lake Iseo — the largest lake island in Southern Europe (4.5 km²). It is car-free, reached by ferry from Sulzano or Sale Marasino, and topped by the sanctuary of the Madonna della Ceriola at 600m. Bikes can be hired on the island.

Is Lake Iseo worth visiting?+

Yes — Lake Iseo is one of the most charming and least crowded of Lombardy's lakes. It offers beautiful scenery, the remarkable Monte Isola island, tranquil villages and a genuinely authentic atmosphere. It combines well with a visit to Bergamo (31km away).

How far is Lake Iseo from Milan?+

Lake Iseo is approximately 90km northeast of Milan — about 1 hour by car.
