Public Transport in Milan

Metro, tram, bus & taxi — single ticket €2.20, valid 90 minutes

Milan's public transport network — operated by ATM (Azienda Trasporti Milanesi) — is efficient, affordable and well-connected. It covers the city with 5 metro lines, 18 tram lines, over 80 bus routes and a night service. A single ticket costs €2.20 and is valid for 90 minutes across all modes.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Tickets and prices

All ATM tickets are integrated — a single ticket is valid on metro, tram and bus within the validity period. Tickets can be bought at metro station vending machines, newsagents, tobacconists (tabaccherie) and via the ATM Milano app. Always validate your ticket before boarding.

💡 Practical tip

If you are staying in Milan's historic centre for 2–3 days and visiting mostly on foot, a handful of single tickets (€2.20 each) will be sufficient. The 24-hour pass (€7.60) is worth it only if you plan to use the metro 4+ times in a day — for instance, if you are going to the airport and back plus visiting outlying attractions.

Getting around — the modes

🚇
Metro
5 lines · 113 stations · 6am–12:30am daily. The fastest way across the city. Connects Linate Airport (M4) and Cadorna (Malpensa Express).
🚋
Tram
18 lines including historic 1920s trams still in service. Runs past midnight. Excellent for Navigli, Corso Buenos Aires and Porta Venezia.
🚌
Bus
80+ lines plus trolley buses. Covers areas not served by metro or tram. Night buses (N lines) run after midnight when metro closes.
🚕
Taxi
Official white taxis use the meter. Start fare ~€3.30 (day) / €6.60 (night/Sunday). Malpensa: fixed €100. Linate: metered, ~€30–40.
🛵
Bike & scooter share
BikeMi docking stations throughout the city. Electric scooters (Lime, Bird) available via app. Practical for short journeys in the centre.
🚎
Sightseeing bus
Hop-on hop-off route covering the main tourist attractions. Useful if you want an overview of the city without walking.

Do tourists need to use public transport?

If you are staying in the historic centre — the area around the Duomo, Brera, Castello Sforzesco and Navigli — most major attractions are within comfortable walking distance and you may need the metro only to reach the airports or distant neighbourhoods. The metro becomes essential for: Linate Airport (M4 from San Babila), Malpensa Express (from Cadorna/Centrale), the Navigli district (M2 Porta Genova), the stadium (M5 San Siro), and trips to outlying areas.

Frequently asked questions

How much is a metro ticket in Milan?+

A single ATM ticket costs €2.20 and is valid for 90 minutes on metro, tram and bus. A 24-hour pass costs €7.60; a 48-hour pass costs €14; a weekly pass €17.

What time does the Milan metro run?+

Daily from 6am to 12:30am (midnight). On 25 December and 1 May: 7am to 7:30pm. On Saturday nights some lines extend to approximately 2am.

How many metro lines does Milan have?+

Milan has 5 metro lines: M1 (red), M2 (green), M3 (yellow), M4 (blue — Linate Airport to San Babila, opened 2023) and M5 (purple). The network covers 94.5km and 113 stations — the longest in Italy.

Do tourists need to use public transport in Milan?+

If staying in the historic centre, most attractions are walkable — you may not need the metro at all. It is most useful for reaching the airports (M4 to Linate; Malpensa Express from Cadorna/Centrale), Navigli (M2), the stadium (M5) and outlying areas.
