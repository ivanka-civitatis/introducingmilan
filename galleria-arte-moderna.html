Galleria d'Arte Moderna

✓ Free entry

The Galleria d'Arte Moderna (GAM) is one of Milan's most underrated museums — a remarkable collection of 19th and 20th-century art, including works by Van Gogh, Cézanne, Picasso and Modigliani, housed free of charge in the elegant 18th-century Villa Reale next to the Giardini Pubblici.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026·⏱️ 3 min read

About the museum

The Galleria d'Arte Moderna is housed in the Villa Reale, a magnificent neoclassical villa built in the late 18th century. The villa still preserves much of its original decoration — gilded rooms, frescoed ceilings and period furniture — which gives the museum an atmosphere quite unlike any other in Milan. It is genuinely beautiful to walk through, independent of the art.

The collection spans from the 18th to the 20th century, with particular strength in Italian Romantic and Symbolist painting. It also contains an important group of international works acquired over the decades, including pieces by some of the most celebrated names in modern European art.

Artists in the collection

The collection includes major works by both Italian and international artists:

🎨 International masters
Van Gogh, Paul Cézanne, Pablo Picasso and Amedeo Modigliani — a distinguished group of works by the defining figures of modern European painting.
🖼️ Italian Romantics
Francesco Hayez, Andrea Appiani and Pompeo Marchesi — key figures of the Italian Romantic movement, many of whom are little known outside Italy.
✨ Italian Symbolists
Tranquillo Cremona, Giovanni Segantini and Giuseppe Pellizza da Volpedo — the generation that reinvented Italian painting in the late 19th century.
🏛️ Padiglione d'Arte Contemporanea
An adjacent pavilion houses the most recent acquisitions — 20th-century works including sculpture and installations moved from the main villa.

Free entry — Milan's best-value museum?

Given the quality of the collection and the extraordinary setting of the Villa Reale, the Galleria d'Arte Moderna may be the best-value museum in Milan. It is free to visit at all times (with additional free-entry days), rarely crowded, and the combination of Van Gogh and Cézanne with the gilded rooms of an 18th-century villa is a genuinely special experience.

Visiting tips

  • Closed on Mondays — plan your visit for Tuesday to Sunday.
  • Free entry on the first Sunday of the month and on the third Tuesday of the month after 2pm (though it is free all the time, these days also waive any temporary exhibition charges).
  • The villa's rooms are worth exploring in their own right for the period decoration — don't rush through them.
  • Combine with the adjacent Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli (169 metres) for a leisurely morning in Porta Venezia.
  • The Padiglione d'Arte Contemporanea is a separate building in the garden — don't miss it.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Galleria d'Arte Moderna in Milan free?+

Yes — the Galleria d'Arte Moderna is free to visit. Open Tuesday to Sunday from 10am to 5:30pm. Closed on Mondays, 1 January, 1 May and 25 December.

What can you see in the Galleria d'Arte Moderna?+

The GAM contains Italian and European art from the 18th to 20th centuries, including works by Van Gogh, Cézanne, Picasso and Modigliani, as well as major Italian artists including Francesco Hayez, Giovanni Segantini and Giuseppe Pellizza da Volpedo. The museum is housed in the spectacular 18th-century Villa Reale.

How do I get to the Galleria d'Arte Moderna?+

By metro: Palestro (line M1), 2-minute walk. By tram: lines 1 and 2 (Piazza Cavour stop). The museum is at Via Palestro, 16, adjacent to the Giardini Pubblici Indro Montanelli.
