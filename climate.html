Climate in Milan

Hot summers, cold foggy winters, beautiful spring and autumn — when to visit for the best experience

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Best time to visit Milan

April to June and September to October are the best times to visit Milan — pleasant temperatures (18–26°C), lower humidity than summer, and the city at its most beautiful. April brings the Salone del Mobile furniture fair, one of the most exciting periods in the city (also one of the most expensive for hotels).

July and August are hot and humid (regularly 30–35°C). Many Milanese leave for the mountains or sea, and some smaller restaurants and shops close. Plan morning sightseeing and afternoon rests.

November to February can be cold, grey and foggy. Milan's infamous fog (la nebbia) reduces visibility in winter. However, there are lower crowds, lower hotel prices and a magical Christmas atmosphere.

🌸 Spring (Mar–May)
12–22°C. Gardens bloom, terraces open. April is excellent. Note: Salone del Mobile in April means higher hotel prices and busier streets.
☀️ Summer (Jun–Aug)
25–35°C, humid. Busiest tourist season. Good for lake day trips. Plan indoor activities in the afternoon heat.
🍂 Autumn (Sep–Oct)
10–22°C. September is ideal — like spring but with full summer operating hours. Fashion Week in September.
❄️ Winter (Nov–Feb)
0–10°C. Cold, grey, foggy. Fewer crowds and better hotel prices. Christmas atmosphere is lovely. Pack warm layers.

Milan weather month by month

January and February are the coldest months — temperatures regularly drop to 0°C or below at night, and Milan's famous fog (la nebbia) can reduce visibility to near zero. The city looks beautiful in the rare moments of winter sun, and the museum queues are minimal. Hotels are at their cheapest.

March and April bring a rapid transformation — blossom, lengthening days, and the return of outdoor terraces. April is one of the best months to visit, but it also coincides with the Salone del Mobile furniture fair, which fills the city and drives hotel prices sharply upward. Book accommodation months in advance if visiting in April.

May and June are the sweet spot — warm (18–28°C), reliably sunny, and not yet overrun. The lakes are at their most beautiful in late May and early June. Fashion Week in February and September is the only caveat — hotels are expensive and fully booked.

July and August can be genuinely hot — 30–35°C with high humidity. The city is partly deserted as Milanese families escape to the mountains or sea. Some smaller restaurants and shops close in August. Air conditioning is not universal in older buildings. That said, the long evenings and outdoor dining make summer visits enjoyable if you plan around the heat.

September and October are arguably the finest months of the year — the summer heat breaks, the lakes are still warm, and the city fills with energy after the August pause. Fashion Week in September brings international visitors but also some excitement. October is excellent for day trips before the lakeside season ends.

November and December bring increasing cold, rain and fog. December is redeemed by the Christmas atmosphere — the O bei! O bei! market at Castello Sforzesco, the Sant'Ambrogio festivities on 7 December, and the opening of the La Scala opera season. The Duomo is particularly beautiful lit up at night.

What to pack for Milan

In summer: light clothing, sunscreen and a bottle for the free drinking fountains throughout the city. Carry a light scarf or layer for churches (shoulders and knees must be covered). In spring and autumn: layers — Milan mornings can be cool even when afternoons are warm. In winter: a proper coat, gloves and scarf. The wind off the Alps can make temperatures feel much colder than the thermometer suggests.

Climate and the Italian Lakes

The lakes modify the local climate significantly. Lake Como and Lake Maggiore create a mild microclimate that allows Mediterranean vegetation — palm trees, bougainvillea, camellias — to thrive at a latitude equivalent to southern Canada. This makes the lakes beautiful in every season and particularly spectacular in spring when the rhododendrons and azaleas bloom. For lake day trips, May, June and September are the best months.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best month to visit Milan?+

May and September are the best individual months — warm, uncrowded (relative to summer) and with the best conditions for lake day trips. April is beautiful but the Salone del Mobile furniture fair fills the city and doubles hotel prices. October is an excellent alternative to September with slightly cooler temperatures and very manageable crowds.

Does it snow in Milan?+

Snow in Milan is relatively rare — the city averages only 10–15 days of snowfall per year, mostly in January and February. When it does snow it rarely settles for more than a day or two in the city itself, though the surrounding mountains are reliably snow-covered from November to March.

Is Milan hot in summer?+

Yes — July and August regularly reach 30–35°C with significant humidity. The Po Valley position traps warm air and makes Milan feel hotter than coastal cities at similar temperatures. The best strategy is to do outdoor sightseeing in the morning, rest or visit air-conditioned museums in the afternoon, and enjoy the long evenings outdoors.

What is Milan like in December?+

December is one of the most atmospheric times to visit Milan — Christmas markets, the Sant'Ambrogio holiday on 7 December, the opening of the La Scala opera season, and the illuminated Duomo. Temperatures are cold (2–7°C) but generally above freezing. Fewer tourists than summer and lower hotel prices.

When should I avoid visiting Milan?+

August is the most challenging month — many locals leave, some restaurants and smaller shops close, and the heat and humidity can be intense. The Salone del Mobile in April and Fashion Week in February and September bring very high hotel prices and crowded streets. These are not reasons to avoid Milan entirely, but worth knowing about when booking.
