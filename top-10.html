Milan Top 10

The 10 unmissable sights every visitor should see

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Milan rewards visitors who go beyond the obvious — but some sights truly cannot be missed on a first visit. Here are the 10 unmissable experiences in and around the city, ranked for a first-time visitor.

1 · Cathedral
Milan Cathedral (Duomo)
Gothic masterpiece · 500 years to build · rooftop with views over the Alps · free interior
2 · Leonardo da Vinci
The Last Supper
The most important painting in Milan — book weeks in advance, only 30 visitors per 15 minutes
3 · Arcade
Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II
19th-century glass arcade · Prada, Gucci, historic cafés · free to enter
4 · Castle
Castello Sforzesco
15th-century Sforza fortress · 7 museums · Michelangelo's Rondanini Pietà
5 · Opera
Teatro alla Scala
The world's most famous opera house · museum open daily · attend a performance
6 · Art gallery
Pinacoteca di Brera
Raphael, Mantegna, Tintoretto, Hayez · 38 rooms · one of Italy's finest collections
7 · Cemetery
Cimitero Monumentale
Open-air sculpture museum · extraordinary 19th-century funerary art · free entry
8 · Medieval square
Piazza Mercanti
Milan's most intact medieval square · 243m from the Duomo · most visitors walk past it
9 · Free museum
Galleria d'Arte Moderna
Van Gogh, Cézanne, Picasso · 18th-century Villa Reale · free entry always
10 · Day trip
Lake Como
Bellagio, Varenna · 50km from Milan · 1 hour by train · Italy's most beautiful lake

A few notes on planning your visit: the Last Supper must be booked as far in advance as possible — tickets sell out weeks or months ahead. The Duomo rooftop can also have queues; book online to skip them. The Cimitero Monumentale and GAM are free and almost always quiet — among the most underrated sights in the city. For Lake Como, late spring and early autumn are the best seasons.

Frequently asked questions

What are the top 10 things to see in Milan?+

The 10 unmissable sights are: (1) Milan Cathedral/Duomo, (2) The Last Supper — book in advance, (3) Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, (4) Castello Sforzesco, (5) Teatro alla Scala, (6) Pinacoteca di Brera, (7) Cimitero Monumentale, (8) Piazza Mercanti, (9) Galleria d'Arte Moderna (free), (10) Lake Como day trip.

What should I not miss in Milan?+

The Last Supper is irreplaceable — book it first. The Duomo rooftop is essential. The Brera gallery deserves 2–3 hours with a guide. For free sights, the Cimitero Monumentale and the GAM are exceptional and almost always empty of tourists.

How much time do I need to see Milan's top 10?+

Two full days covers the main sights comfortably if well planned. Day 1: Duomo, Galleria, La Scala, Brera. Day 2: Last Supper, Castello, Piazza Mercanti, GAM or Cimitero, Navigli. Add a third day for Lake Como. See our 48-hour itinerary.
