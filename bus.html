Buses in Milan

80+ ATM lines · night buses after midnight · same ticket as metro (€2.20)

Milan's bus network complements the metro and trams, with over 80 ATM urban bus lines plus a small number of trolleybus routes. Buses serve areas not covered by rail, and the night bus network (N lines) keeps running after midnight when the metro and trams close. The standard ATM ticket (€2.20, 90 minutes) is valid on all buses.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Key bus lines for tourists

🚌 Bus 73 – Linate Airport
Linate Airport → San Babila metro. Every 10 minutes throughout the day, 6:05am–12:40am. Standard ATM ticket. Since 2023, Metro M4 is faster (15 min vs ~25 min), but bus 73 remains a valid option.
🚌 Bus 94 – City centre
Runs through the southern part of the city centre including Duomo area and Via Torino. Useful for areas between metro stops.
🚌 Trolleybus 90/91 – Outer ring
The 90/91 circular routes form Milan's outer ring, connecting many neighbourhoods outside the metro network. Useful for reaching areas not served by rail.
🌙 Night buses (N lines)
After midnight, N-prefixed night buses replace trams and metro on the main routes. Frequency approximately every 30 minutes. Same ATM ticket valid.

How to use Milan buses

Board at any door — buses in Milan do not have a fixed boarding door. Validate your ticket immediately at the yellow validator on board. Inspectors operate regularly and fines for unticketed travel are €60–€100.

Tickets cannot be bought on most buses — purchase in advance at metro station vending machines, newsagents, tobacconists (tabaccherie) or via the ATM Milano app. Where a driver-sale is possible, a supplement applies.

💡 Bus 73 vs Metro M4 to Linate

Bus 73 to Linate Airport remains useful if you are already on Corso Buenos Aires or the eastern part of the city and don't want to backtrack to a metro station. But if you're coming from the Duomo area or central Milan, the Metro M4 from San Babila is faster (15 min) and equally cheap (€2.20). Check your location before deciding.

Frequently asked questions

How do I get from Linate Airport to Milan by bus?+

Bus 73 runs from Linate to San Babila metro every 10 minutes, ATM ticket (€2.20). Since 2023, Metro M4 also runs the same route in 15 minutes and is generally the better option from central Milan.

Do Milan buses run at night?+

Yes — after midnight, night bus N lines replace trams and metro on the main routes, approximately every 30 minutes. The standard ATM ticket (€2.20) is valid.

How much is a bus ticket in Milan?+

Standard ATM ticket: €2.20, valid 90 minutes on bus, tram and metro. Validate on board before the bus moves. See all ticket options →
