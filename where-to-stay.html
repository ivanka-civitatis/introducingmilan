Where to Stay in Milan

Best areas for tourists — Duomo, Brera, Navigli, Porta Venezia & more

Milan is a compact city and most major attractions are within walking distance of the centre — so your choice of neighbourhood matters less here than in cities like Rome or Paris. That said, where you stay shapes the texture of your visit, and some areas are significantly more atmospheric, convenient or good value than others.

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Best areas to stay in Milan

⛪ Duomo / Centro Storico Luxury–Mid
The most central option — within walking distance of the Duomo, Galleria, La Scala and most major attractions. Hotels here tend to be expensive, but the convenience is unbeatable for first-time visitors with limited time.
✓ Walking distance to everything ✓ Excellent for 1–2 night visits · ✗ Most expensive area · ✗ Tourist-heavy
🎨 Brera Luxury–Mid
Milan's most elegant and atmospheric neighbourhood — cobblestone streets, boutiques, excellent restaurants and the Pinacoteca di Brera. A beautiful place to be based, slightly quieter than the Duomo area. Strong hotel options at all price points.
✓ Atmospheric · ✓ Great restaurants · ✓ Close to Duomo (15 min walk) · ✗ Still expensive
🚢 Navigli Mid-range
Milan's canal district — the most lively and bohemian area, best for nightlife, aperitivo and a younger vibe. Slightly further from the main sights (20 min walk or M2 to Porta Genova). Good range of mid-range hotels and B&Bs.
✓ Lively atmosphere · ✓ Great aperitivo scene · ✓ Good value · ✗ Noisy at weekends
🌳 Porta Venezia / Buenos Aires Good value
A well-connected, multicultural neighbourhood with excellent transport links (M1 Lima/Loreto), Corso Buenos Aires shopping, and the Giardini Pubblici. More local in character than the historic centre, with competitive hotel prices. Metro M4 connects directly to Linate Airport.
✓ Good value · ✓ Excellent metro connections · ✓ Linate Airport (M4) · ✓ GAM museum nearby
🚂 Centrale / Repubblica Budget–Mid
Practical rather than atmospheric — excellent for transport (high-speed trains, Malpensa Express, multiple metro lines) but the neighbourhood itself is more functional than charming. Best suited to travellers doing day trips or with early/late trains.
✓ Perfect transport hub · ✓ Budget options available · ✗ Less atmospheric · ✗ Can feel impersonal
🌿 Isola / Porta Garibaldi Mid-range
The trendiest neighbourhood in contemporary Milan — creative restaurants, independent shops, the Porta Nuova skyscraper district (Gae Aulenti) and excellent transport (M2/M5 Garibaldi). Increasingly popular with design-aware visitors.
✓ Trendy and creative · ✓ Excellent restaurants · ✓ Gae Aulenti area · ✓ Good metro connections
📅 Watch out for Fashion Week & trade fairs

Milan hosts two Fashion Weeks per year (February and September) and year-round trade fairs at the Fiera Milano, including the enormous Salone del Mobile furniture fair in April. During these periods, hotel prices can double or triple and availability drops sharply. If your dates overlap with a major event, book several months in advance.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best area to stay in Milan?+

For first-time visitors: Duomo/Centro Storico (most convenient, most expensive) or Brera (elegant, atmospheric). For value: Porta Venezia. For nightlife: Navigli. For transport: Centrale.

Is Milan expensive for accommodation?+

Yes — Milan is one of the more expensive Italian cities for hotels. Prices spike sharply during Fashion Week (February/September) and major trade fairs. Book well in advance for these periods. Porta Venezia and Centrale offer the best value.

Is it worth staying near Milano Centrale station?+

Practical for transport connections (trains, airport buses, metro M2/M3) but less atmospheric. Good choice if you are doing day trips or have early/late trains. For a more immersive Milan experience, Brera or Navigli are better.
