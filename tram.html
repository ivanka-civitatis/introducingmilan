Trams in Milan

18 lines · runs past midnight daily · same ATM ticket as metro (€2.20)

Milan's tram network — one of the oldest in Italy, with routes running since 1876 — covers 18 lines across the city, including several that still operate with historic 1920s rolling stock. The distinctive orange-and-cream vintage trams are among Milan's most recognisable sights. All trams use the standard ATM ticket (€2.20, valid 90 minutes).

✍️ By Ivanka Garcia Mancebo·Updated June 2026

Key lines for tourists

🚋 Line 1 – City centre loop
Runs through the heart of the city — useful for Castello Sforzesco, Largo Cairoli and the Duomo area. Often operated by historic trams.
🚋 Line 2 – Via Manzoni
Connects Piazza Cavour, Via Manzoni (Poldi Pezzoli Museum) and the central shopping area. Stop for Teatro alla Scala.
🚋 Line 9 – Centrale to Porta Genova
Useful north–south line connecting Milano Centrale to the Navigli district (Porta Genova). Fast alternative to M2 for Navigli.
🚋 Line 16 – Stazione Centrale
Runs from Centrale through the city centre to Corso Buenos Aires — one of Milan's main shopping streets. Often historic trams.
🚋 Line 19 – Navigli & Sempione
Connects Navigli with the city centre and continues to Parco Sempione / Triennale. Good for combining a Navigli evening with a daytime Sempione visit.
🚋 Line 24 – Brera to Porta Venezia
Connects the Brera area with Corso Buenos Aires and Porta Venezia (GAM museum, Giardini Pubblici). Useful for the northeast of the centre.

Hours, frequency and tickets

Trams run daily from approximately 6am to midnight. After midnight, night bus services (N lines) replace trams on most routes. Frequency varies from every 5 minutes on busy central routes to every 15–20 minutes on outlying lines.

The standard ATM ticket (€2.20) is valid for 90 minutes on trams, buses and one metro journey. Always validate your ticket at the yellow validator on board before the tram moves — not doing so counts as travelling without a valid ticket and is subject to fines of €60–€100. A supplement applies if you buy a ticket from the driver after boarding.

🏛️ The historic trams

Milan's vintage orange trams — known as Carrelli — date from the 1920s and 1930s and are still in daily service on several central lines. More than 160 of these vehicles remain in operation, making Milan one of the few cities in the world where historic trams run as regular public transport, not tourist attractions. If you spot one, hop on.

Frequently asked questions

How much is a tram ticket in Milan?+

The standard ATM ticket costs €2.20, valid for 90 minutes on tram, bus and metro. Validate at the yellow machine on board before the tram moves. Buying from the driver incurs a supplement.

What time do Milan trams run?+

Daily from approximately 6am to midnight. After midnight, night bus (N) services replace trams on most routes.

Are there historic trams in Milan?+

Yes — Milan still runs historic 1920s trams (Carrelli) as regular public transport on several central lines, including lines 1 and 16. Over 160 vintage trams remain in service.
